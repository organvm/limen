================================================================================
KEY INSIGHTS FOR COMPOSITION
================================================================================

1. GRENDEL'S PRESENCE EXTENDS BEYOND FITT 2
   While Fitt 2 establishes Grendel, his occupation continues as an unspoken
   background through Fitts 3–5. The music should carry harmonic memory of
   Fitt 2's dissonance.

2. THE EMOTIONAL CENTER IS FITT 3
   This is where the Danes reach their deepest despair. The Fitt 3 nadir is
   the valley that gives meaning to both Fitts 2 (the source of the crisis)
   and Fitts 4–5 (the answering).

3. LAW AND PROTOCOL ARE STRUCTURAL VALUES
   Fitt 5's insistence on proper form (coast-guard challenges, Beowulf answers
   courteously) is not decoration. It is the poem's claim that order, even under
   mortal threat, is what holds civilization together.

4. TRANSITION MOMENTS SHAPE THE ARC
   - Fitt 2→3: From eruption to helplessness (the question: can this be endured?)
   - Fitt 3→4: From nadir to hope (Beowulf hears; the hero answers)
   - Fitt 4→5: From passage to threshold (the hero arrives; law tests him)

5. THE STRUCTURE IS NATURALLY FOUR-MOVEMENT
   (I) Dissonant, isolated monster-music (Fitt 2)
   (II) Exhausted, dirge-like civic collapse shifting to ascending heroic motif (Fitt 3)
   (III) Resolute, rhythmic seafaring march with triumphant arrival (Fitt 4)
   (IV) Formal, ceremonial threshold-crossing with protocol honored (Fitt 5)
