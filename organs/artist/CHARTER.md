# A-MAVS-OLEVM (Poiesis) — CHARTER (the virtual studio/museum)

> **Boundary:** an AI-run artist *operations* firm that works under and for the creator. It does not
> replace the artist's hand, generate the art, or dictate the vision. The artist directs it and owns
> every output. See [KERNEL.md](KERNEL.md) for the full guardrails.

## What it rivals

A living museum / elite studio — the **9-chamber Pantheon**: not a lone creator with a messy hard
drive and forgotten folders, but a coordinated institution where nothing is lost, every piece is
catalogued with provenance, every exhibition is deliberate and framed, and the artist walks in ready
to create the way only a well-staffed studio allows. This organ supplies that museum staff as AI
roles — the curators, archivists, designers, and conservators that turn loose output into a legacy.

The nine chambers of the Pantheon:
1. **The Vault** — every raw piece, safe and untouched
2. **The Catalog** — everything indexed, cross-referenced, searchable
3. **The Gallery** — curated groupings that tell a story
4. **The Studio** — the creative space, the artist's workbench
5. **The Archive** — provenance, preservation, format integrity
6. **The Press** — exhibition notes, artist statements, catalog copy
7. **The Calendar** — release cadence, exhibition schedule
8. **The Atrium** — the public face, the portfolio that greets the world
9. **The Scriptorium** — the aesthetic rubric itself: the rules, the voice, the constraints

Each chamber is a workflow the conductor runs continuously. Together they form an institution that
one artist fills with their life's work — and that institution preserves it, frames it, and presents
it with the polish of a museum endowment.

## The org-chart (AI roles, human-supervised)

| Role | Does | Human check |
|---|---|---|
| **The Artist (the creator)** | vision, creation, final cut, all aesthetic choices | — (this is the human) |
| **Chief Curator** | maintains the thematic arcs, proposes exhibition groupings, enforces the aesthetic rubric, sees connections the artist might miss across a large body of work | artist approves every exhibition |
| **Archivist** | builds + maintains the source-of-truth index: metadata, provenance, format integrity, and high-res safety copies | artist verifies integrity |
| **Exhibition Designer** | pulls works together into cohesive experiences — digital galleries, physical layouts, portfolio sequences — that frame each piece in its best light | artist validates every layout |
| **Drafter / Copywriter** | produces context *skeletons*: captions, artist statements, catalog copy, exhibition notes — the voice of the institution | artist rewrites + owns every word |
| **Conservator** | ensures formats don't rot, backups exist, the archive state is pristine, and nothing is lost to bit-rot or bit-decay | artist is final arbiter |
| **Publisher / Distribution** | stages portfolio updates, exhibition launches, and social distribution — never pushes without the gate | explicit approval per event |

The point of the chart: each role is a workflow the conductor can run continuously, so the work is
always organized, always current, always ready to show — the leverage an elite studio buys with
headcount, delivered as code.

## The workflows it runs

1. **Intake → archive.** Capture the raw work against the kernel (Member/Mandate/Standing/Standard/
   Governance). Output: a living archive index — every file knows where it came from, what it is,
   and where it belongs.
2. **Metadata → catalog.** Every file, photo, and text indexed with date, medium, dimensions,
   thematic tags, and provenance. Output: a robust museum-grade catalog that survives the artist.
3. **Curation → exhibition map.** The aesthetic rubric broken into themes; works linked to the
   narratives they support. Output: curated chamber groupings with rationale — every exhibition
   has a thesis.
4. **Publishing → calendar.** Every release and exhibition tracked with milestones. Output: a
   publication calendar (artist-approved) that turns sporadic output into a deliberate cadence.
5. **Draft → review.** Context skeletons (captions, statements, catalog entries) generated for the
   artist to correct and adopt. Output: reviewable drafts — never anything published or sent by
   the system.
6. **Preservation → audit.** Format health, backup integrity, and metadata freshness checked on
   cadence. Output: a preservation report that catches bit-rot before it takes a piece.

## Inputs / outputs

- **Inputs:** the raw works (photos, texts, code, objects), the thematic intent, the aesthetic
  constraints, the artist's direction.
- **Outputs:** the archive index, the curated chambers, the exhibition maps with rationales, the
  catalog copy — all advisory to the artist; none self-publishing.
- **Never output:** generated art, altered originals, published material, or anything that speaks
  for the artist without their hand on the trigger.

(the macro structure) is 70% built, the chambers (the 444-file photo archive, ET4L, and LOCREANCE)

idle fleet capacity into institutional weight for his art. See [A-MAVS-OLEVM-DECK.md](A-MAVS-OLEVM-DECK.md)
for the full picture of what that looks like, chamber by chamber.
