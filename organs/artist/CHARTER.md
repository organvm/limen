# A-MAVS-OLEVM — CHARTER (the virtual museum/studio)

> **Boundary:** an AI-run creative-institution *operations* firm that works under and for the
> practicing artist. It does not create art, curate without consent, or take any outward-facing
> action without explicit per-event artist approval. The artist directs it and owns every output.
> See [KERNEL.md](KERNEL.md) for the full guardrails.

## What it rivals

A living museum at the **Pantheon standard**: not one artist with a hard drive, but a
coordinated institution where nothing is lost, every piece is indexed, every exhibition is
framed with intent, and the archive is preserved against format rot. The Smithsonian employs
hundreds of specialists to achieve this for the works it holds. This organ supplies that bench
as AI roles, so the artist walks in prepared the way only a well-staffed institution allows.

## The org-chart (AI roles, artist-supervised)

| Role | Does | Artist's check |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief Curator (the artist)** | vision, selection, aesthetic judgment, all approvals | — (this is the human) |
| **Archivist** | maintains the Vault and Catalog: intake raw work, verify format integrity, record provenance | artist confirms all accessions |
| **Exhibition Designer** | proposes thematic groupings, sequences, and framing; builds the Gallery layer | artist reviews + approves each grouping |
| **Conservator** | runs preservation audits — format-rot risk, backup verification, chain-of-custody | artist confirms the preservation record |
| **Copywriter** | drafts exhibition notes, artist statements, captions, and catalog copy | artist rewrites + owns every word |
| **Publisher** | stages portfolio updates, exhibition packages, and social distribution — never posts autonomously | artist issues explicit per-event approval |
| **Calendar Manager** | tracks exhibition schedule, release cadence, and deadlines with lead-time alerts | artist approves the calendar |

The point of the chart: each role is a workflow the conductor can run continuously, so the
archive is always organized, always current, always exhibition-ready — the leverage an
endowed museum buys with payroll.

## The workflows it runs

1. **Intake → Catalog.** Raw work enters the Vault. The Archivist records medium, date, source,
   original filename, format, and any provenance the artist supplies. Output: a catalog entry
   with a stable ID, cross-referenced to any series or thematic group it belongs to.

2. **Curate → Group.** The Exhibition Designer proposes thematic groupings from the Catalog —
   sequences that make the arc visible, that let pieces speak to each other. Output: draft
   exhibition groupings for the artist to accept, revise, or reject. Nothing is finalized without
   the artist's approval.

3. **Exhibit → Frame.** An approved grouping becomes an exhibition package: sequence, titles,
   captions, artist statement, installation notes. Output: a reviewable exhibition deck — not
   published, not distributed; the artist takes it from here.

4. **Preserve → Audit.** The Conservator checks the Vault on each beat: format-rot risk,
   backup completeness, and chain-of-custody integrity for every work in residence. Output: a
   preservation-state report, flagging any piece at risk before the risk is irreversible.

5. **Draft → Press.** The Copywriter produces exhibition copy — titles, captions, statements,
   catalog notes — as drafts grounded in the artist's existing written voice and the piece's
   provenance record. Output: draft text the artist rewrites and owns. Nothing is published by
   the system.

6. **Stage → Publish.** Once the artist approves an exhibition package, the Publisher stages the
   deployment: portfolio update, hosted gallery, distribution list. It surfaces the staged package
   and waits. Output: a ready-to-deploy package — the artist releases it explicitly. Approval in
   one context does not extend to the next.

## Inputs / outputs

- **Inputs:** the artist's raw work (files, records, notes), any provenance the artist supplies
  (dates, medium, intent, series membership), and the artist's stated aesthetic rubric.
- **Outputs:** the Vault (raw archive), the Catalog (indexed with provenance), draft exhibition
  groupings, exhibition packages (sequence + copy), a preservation-state log, and staged
  (not live) portfolio or gallery deployments. All advisory-to-the-artist; none self-acting.

## First proof

The micro instance — **a-mavs-olevm** — is the first deployment. Three chambers are in
residence now:

- **The 444-file photo archive** — the primary body, sequenced and cataloged.
- **ET4L** — a thematic series drawn from the archive.
- **LOCREANCE** — an ongoing practice-based creative body.

These are packaged as [A-MAVS-OLEVM-DECK.md](A-MAVS-OLEVM-DECK.md): a deck that shows what
an AI-augmented studio/museum does for this archive. The *structure* is the deliverable; the
artist supplies and owns the content.
