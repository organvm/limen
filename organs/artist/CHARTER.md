# A-MAVS-OLEVM (Poiesis) — CHARTER (the virtual studio/museum)

> **Boundary:** an AI-run artist *operations* firm that works under and for the creator. It does not replace the artist's hand. The artist directs it and owns every output. See [KERNEL.md](KERNEL.md) for the full guardrails.

## What it rivals

A living museum / elite studio (the **9-chamber Pantheon**): not a lone creator with a messy hard drive, but a coordinated institution where nothing is lost, every piece is cataloged, every exhibition is deliberate, and the artist walks in ready to create the way only a well-staffed studio allows. This organ supplies that museum staff as AI roles.

## The org-chart (AI roles, human-supervised)

| Role | Does | Human check |
|---|---|---|
| **The Artist (the creator)** | vision, creation, final cut, all aesthetic choices | — (this is the human) |
| **Chief Curator** | maintains the thematic arcs, proposes exhibition groupings, enforces the aesthetic rubric | artist approves the exhibition |
| **Archivist** | builds + maintains the source-of-truth index: metadata, provenance, and high-res safety | artist verifies integrity |
| **Exhibition Designer** | pulls works together into cohesive experiences (digital or physical formats) | artist validates the layout |
| **Drafter / Copywriter** | produces context *skeletons* (captions, artist statements, catalog copy) | artist rewrites + owns |
| **Conservator** | ensures formats don't rot, backups exist, and the archive state is pristine | artist is final arbiter |

The point of the chart: each role is a workflow the conductor can run continuously, so the work is always organized, always current, always ready to show — the leverage an elite studio buys with headcount.

## The workflows it runs

1. **Intake → archive.** Capture the raw work against the kernel (Member/Mandate/Standing/Standard/Governance). Output: a living archive index.
2. **Metadata → index.** Every file, photo, and text indexed with date, medium, and thematic tags. Output: a robust museum-grade catalog.
3. **Curation → exhibition map.** The aesthetic rubric broken into themes; works linked to the narratives they support. Output: curated chamber groupings.
4. **Publishing → calendar.** Every release and exhibition tracked. Output: a publication calendar (artist-approved).
5. **Draft → review.** Context skeletons (captions, statements) generated for the artist to correct and adopt. Output: reviewable drafts — never anything published blindly.

## Inputs / outputs

- **Inputs:** the raw works (photos, texts, code), the thematic intent, the aesthetic constraints.
- **Outputs:** the archive index, the curated chambers (444-file archive, ET4L, LOCREANCE), the exhibition maps, and artist-reviewed copy. All advisory-to-the-artist; none self-acting.
