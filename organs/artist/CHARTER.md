# A-MAVS-OLEVM — CHARTER (the virtual museum/studio)

> **Boundary:** this is AI-run creative-institution infrastructure that augments a practicing artist.
> It does **not** create art, curate without approval, or publish autonomously. The artist directs,
approves, and owns every output.
> See [KERNEL.md](KERNEL.md) for the full map and guardrails.

## What it rivals

A living museum does this for a major artist: it makes a body of work findable, sequenced, and exhibition-ready.
A-MAVS-OLEVM supplies that with AI roles and explicit artist authority.

| Role | What it does | Artist check |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief Curator (the artist)** | vision, selection, aesthetic judgment, publication approvals | — (final authority) |
| **Archivist** | maintains the Vault and Catalog; records provenance and format integrity | artist confirms all accessions |
| **Exhibition Designer** | builds coherent groupings and sequencing logic | artist reviews and approves each package |
| **Conservator** | tracks format-risk and preservation health | artist confirms preservation reports |
| **Copywriter** | drafts titles, captions, statements, and catalog notes | artist rewrites and owns language |
| **Publisher** | stages portfolio updates and distribution bundles | artist gives explicit per-event approval |
| **Calendar Manager** | tracks exhibition cadence and deadlines | artist approves schedule and timing |

The point is not labor substitution. The point is institutional continuity: work stays ordered under one human
intellect with one coherent infrastructure.

## The workflows it runs

1. **Intake → Vault.** Raw work enters with metadata capture (source, medium, date, provenance links).
2. **Catalog.** The Archivist creates stable records and cross-references for each piece.
3. **Curate → Group.** Exhibition Designer proposes sequences and arc-logic for review.
4. **Exhibit → Frame.** Approved groupings become exhibition packages with drafts for framing language.
5. **Preserve → Audit.** Conservator issues a preservation-status report and risk log.
6. **Draft → Press.** Copywriter produces publication-ready drafts under the artist-authored rubric.
7. **Stage → Publish.** Publisher prepares staged releases; no publish until explicit event-level approval.

## Inputs / outputs

- **Inputs:** raw work, provenance notes, aesthetic rubric, and explicit artist direction.
- **Outputs:** Vault entries, catalog, stable proposals for groupings, draft press text, preservation logs,
and staged deployment packs that the artist can approve, revise, or reject.

## Boundaries (always)

- No AI-generated output is inserted into the archive as finished work.
- Original source files are never rewritten by the organ.
- Provenance is treated as source truth.
- No publication or social release without explicit approval.
- Every draft is advisory until the artist publishes.
