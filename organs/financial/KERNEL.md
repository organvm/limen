# Financial Office — KERNEL (Aerarium)

> **Boundary (load-bearing, repeated everywhere in this organ):** this is financial-operations
> infrastructure that AUGMENTS human financial judgment, stewardship, and decision-making. It does
> **not** execute trades, move money, sign contracts, file taxes, or open accounts autonomously.
> Every disbursement, investment, tax filing, account opening, contractual commitment, and
> beneficiary designation stays with the human who owns the wealth. The organ tracks, models,
> projects, reconciles, alerts, drafts, and schedules — so one person can hold the institutional
> weight of a family office without pretending the machine is the fiduciary.

## Purpose

This is an **institutional prosthesis** for financial life: a reusable family-office subsystem
that makes financial stewardship durable across all the domains money touches — revenue,
expenses, investments, taxes, disbursements, obligations, and generational goals — without
moving money itself.

## Why this organ exists

The rich do not manage their own money. Behind every billionaire's personal wealth stands a
family office: CPAs, tax strategists, investment analysts, bill-pay clerks, estate planners,
insurance brokers, and a chief financial officer who connects the pieces. The numbers are
always current, the tax position is always optimized, the bills are always paid, and
opportunities are always surfaced — not because the principal is a financial genius, but
because the institutional bench never sleeps.

Everyone else runs their financial life from a personal checking account, a shoebox of
receipts, a QuickBooks file updated quarterly, and a panicked April 14th. The difference
between those modes is not intelligence — it is **institutional weight**.

This organ gives one person, and then anyone, the infrastructure of a billionaire's family
office: a continuously reconciled, always-current, projection-capable view of their entire
financial position, with AI roles that do what a CPO, tax strategist, analyst, and bill-pay
clerk would do — without the machine ever touching the money itself.

This organ was named by Anthony as **Aerarium** (the public treasury of Rome, the state
chest). It is generically archival at the base (anyone's financial position) and
instance-first atop that base: **generic + nameless underneath, his instance on top**.

SCALING CONSTRAINT: This organ evolves the-invisible-ledger from a B2B CPA tool into a
personal family office. Every artifact, workflow, and role bends toward that end.

## The 5-primitive kernel, mapped to the financial domain

| Primitive | Financial meaning | Concretely |
|---|---|---|
| **Member** | **account / entity** | the financial person, entity, account, or instrument: identity, jurisdiction, ownership, access boundaries, authorized signers, and what it is for |
| **Mandate** | **goal / allocation** | the financial purpose being served: spending need, savings target, investment thesis, tax strategy, disbursement rule, estate intent, or liquidity requirement |
| **Standing** | **net position** | current financial state aggregated across all members: balance, cash flow, net worth, obligations, runway, tax exposure, and open decisions |
| **Standard** | **tax / compliance rule** | the governing rules: tax code, accounting standards, regulatory requirements, fiduciary duties, disbursement policies, and risk limits that constrain what can be done |
| **Governance** | **the office's controls** | who may see or spend what, approval gates, disbursement thresholds, audit trail requirements, segregation of duties, and the boundary between advice and action |

This is the *same* kernel as the legal, education, governance, health, and social organs.
Only the domain skin changes — and the irreducible constraint that money moves only on the
human's hand.

## Fractal deployment

- **MACRO** — [a family-office-in-a-box anyone can deploy](MACRO.md): multi-entity position tracking,
  cash-flow projection, tax-position estimation, disbursement scheduling, obligation ledger,
  goal-based allocation tracking, and customizable governance rules. Runs on
  the-invisible-ledger as its core accounting engine. The macro form is intentionally
  generic — no personal balances, no private entity names, no real account numbers at the
  template layer.

- **MICRO** — [Anthony's own family office](MICRO.md): product revenue disbursement (institutional +
  personal via payrail), personal balance-sheet + cashflow, wealth/portfolio tracking,
  tax automation, obligations ledger, and ADA-matter-related financial planning. The micro
  instance proves the macro platform by running Anthony's real finances through the same
  workflows that serve any user.

## First artifact

The first working slice is a **personal balance-sheet + cashflow + payrail disbursement map**
run on Anthony's real numbers:

1. Entity registry: personal accounts, business entities (LLC, non-profit), KYC state, and
   access boundaries for each.
2. Balance sheet: net worth across all entities, updated continuously from connected sources
   (or manual baseline where automation is unavailable).
3. Cash-flow projection: inbound (product revenue, grants, consulting) and outbound (bills,
   subscriptions, disbursements, payroll) on a rolling 12-week horizon.
4. Payrail disbursement map: how money flows from revenue source → institutional accounts →
   personal accounts → obligations, with the legal/tax scaffolding for each hop.
5. Obligations ledger: recurring commitments, upcoming tax payments, insurance premiums, and
   their deadline calendar.

Each component starts as a document (Markdown, updated by the organ each beat) and graduates
to a live dashboard as the-invisible-ledger scales.

## Hard guardrails (every contributor + every dispatched task)

- No money movement. Disbursements, trades, transfers, bill-pay, and tax filing are drafted,
  scheduled, and staged — the human executes or does not execute in their bank/broker/tax
  portal.
- No account access. Credentials, API keys, and banking tokens are never stored or handled
  by the organ. The organ reads from manual reports, statement uploads, and human-provided
  summaries where direct integration is unavailable.
- No legal/tax advice. Projections, estimates, and alerts inform the human's decisions; they
  are not filings, returns, or binding positions.
- Privacy is structural. Financial data stays with the owning entity; access is scoped by
  governance rules, not convenience.
- All projections are labeled with their assumptions and confidence level. No number appears
  without its caveat.
- The human's hand is the only authorization for any outward financial act. This organ
  prepares and alerts; it does not authorize or execute.
