## Decisions (his-hand)

**Corrected 2026-07-09 (operator feedback + token gauge).** The first pass treated the non-Claude
subs as redundant overlap. Wrong frame for this fleet: **vendor subs are parallel capacity pools,
not duplicate chat apps** (standing doctrine: use all vendors, never serialize). The Claude Max
pool measurably exhausts in ~2 days of its weekly window; the other pools carry the remaining
days. At the measured 111x arbitrage, the **binding constraint is capacity, not spend** — a $200
pool that absorbs frontier work when Claude is dry is cheap capacity, not waste. Cancel only a
pool that utilization data shows persistently idle; never cancel a pool that hits its caps.

**The real relief for the 2-day Claude exhaustion is routing, not cancellation:** ~10.4% of Claude
tokens are haiku-class (see [`token-usage.md`](token-usage.md)) — moving that tier onto the
now-live qwen3:8b floor and open-weight APIs (plan Phase 2) stretches the Max window at $0–pennies,
and every other pool's capacity goes further for the same reason.

**Non-decisions (already settled by the plan):** Claude Max stays (fleet-native, 111x measured);
OpenCode Go stays (cheapest live lane); Cloudflare stays (runtime infra); no auto-refill on any
credits product.
