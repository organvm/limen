# Recovery & Access Protocol: Post-Injury

> **Boundary (load-bearing):** This is health-operations infrastructure. It tracks adherence and coordinates care, but does **not** provide medical advice, diagnosis, or clinical judgment. The patient and their licensed care team are the sole authorities on treatment.

## Context
- **Event:** Post-injury recovery tracking (4th-story fall, ~2024).
- **Purpose:** Serve as the structural prosthesis for the patient, ensuring protocol adherence and translating medical constraints into institutional access rights (ADA accommodation).

## 1. Active Mandates (The Protocol)
*Tracked prescribed routines, therapy cadence, and specialist directives.*

**Physical Therapy & Mobility:**
- **Cadence:** 3x weekly physical therapy sessions.
- **Constraints:** Weight-bearing limits as specified by the orthopedic specialist; gradual exposure to load; monitored joint stress.
- **Tracking:** Every session and home exercise routine is logged (see `cases/01-post-injury/protocol-log.md`).

**Neurological / Cognitive Load:**
- **Constraints:** Managed screen time, cognitive pacing to prevent fatigue, and strict adherence to rest intervals.

**Surgical / Specialist Follow-up:**
- **Cadence:** Bi-weekly orthopedic reviews, monthly neuro-evaluations.

## 2. Institutional Access & Accommodations (ADA)
*Translating the medical protocol into the legal organ's domain.*

**Workplace Accommodations (Legal Organ Link):**
- **Remote Parity:** Mandatory remote work flexibility on high-pain or physical therapy days.
- **Ergonomic Mandates:** Specialized seating and desk arrangements compliant with orthopedic constraints.
- **Schedule Flexibility:** Non-linear work hours to accommodate therapy sessions and cognitive pacing requirements.

**Escalation Trigger:**
- Any institutional pushback on the above accommodations triggers a hand-off to the **Legal Organ**, fueled by the continuous evidence generated by this protocol (via the `posture.md` brief).

## 3. Workflow Implementation
- **Intake:** Clinician directives are translated into tracking items.
- **Active:** The protocol log records every exercise, medication, and appointment. 
- **Review:** The `posture.md` brief is generated weekly, consolidating state, protocol adherence, and accommodation status. This brief is shared with new providers to avoid "starting from scratch" and serves as evidence for the ADA matter.
