# Sovereign Systems — KERNEL (ORGANVM-as-a-service)

> **Boundary (load-bearing, repeated everywhere in this organ):** this is consulting-infrastructure that
augments human founders, operators, and domain specialists. It does **not** replace commercial judgment,
sales judgment, final scope decisions, legal advice, invoicing, contract execution, or signature authority.
The human partner owns every external act, every commitment, and every outward-facing statement.

## Why this organ exists

Most teams with good intent lose to well-backed teams because they lack institutional memory, fixed
delivery routines, and a consistent operating model. The wealthy have firms, PM systems, and staff that
convert work into reliable outcomes. The solo operator has context and will, but not always the same floor
under the feet.

This organ gives one person the institutional weight of a boutique service studio: structured intake, explicit
goals and scope, disciplined delivery evidence, and a reusable operations layer that can be held by anyone.
The same scaffold serves `Sovereign Systems` now and can serve future clients later without being tuned
only to one personality.

## The 5-primitive kernel, mapped to the consulting domain

| Primitive | Consulting meaning | Concretely |
|---|---|---|
| **Member** | client | the person or organization requesting work; its constraints, context, and consent |
| **Mandate** | engagement | what outcome was hired for, in language the client can sign |
| **Standing** | delivery posture | where the engagement sits now: discovery, proposal, acceptance, execution, review, or hold |
| **Standard** | quality bar | minimum thresholds for clarity, evidence quality, scope integrity, and handoff completeness |
| **Governance** | authority and ethics | who decides, what requires explicit approval, what can’t be sent or promised by the system |

This is the same kernel as other maturing organs — one structure, every pillar.

## Fractal deployment

- **MACRO** — `ORGANVM-as-a-service`: a reusable consulting toolkit any operator can hold:
  - structured intake and engagement capture,
  - a canonical client posture record (Member/Mandate/Standing),
  - proposal-to-delivery transformation templates,
  - quality gates for each deliverable stage,
  - evidence-first project indexing,
  - and a clean archive of what was promised, built, and still open.

  The macro form is intentionally generic. It avoids hardcoded names, private constraints, and private pricing
  assumptions. It is the institutional platform an outside founder can adopt.

- **MICRO** — `the Maddie / Rob / Derek client deployments`: the current manual prototype face of this
  organ, now running across three named deployment threads. It proves the macro platform by forcing the same
  process to hold in three different real situations:
  - **Maddie** — engagement intake and delivery posture under changing scope.
  - **Rob** — recurring execution support with staged deadlines and clear decision checkpoints.
  - **Derek** — cross-program work with frequent context shifts, testing portability of the posture model.

  These are not autonomous clients. Each one is a manual pilot, with all external actions passing human review.

## Hard constraints for this beat (non-negotiable)

- **Manual prototype mode only.** No autonomous client messaging, no autonomous contract send, no
  autonomous billing, and no autonomous commitment of external obligations.
- **No irreversible outward actions without his hand.** If there is a send, signature, acceptance, or public
  deliverable, it is staged and surfaced for human action.
- **No overreach.** This is delivery infrastructure, not legal, tax, or medical advice.
- **Scope integrity.** Every engagement has a concrete current standing, and scope changes must be explicit.
- **No invented proof.** Deliverables and status are what actually exists in this repo and on human-approved
  channels; placeholders are labeled as such.

## Working definition of a healthy micro instance

An engagement is healthy when:

- context is captured in one place,
- scope is explicit and versioned,
- each milestone has a named owner and a human gate,
- there is a clear quality standard per deliverable,
- and all handoff artifacts are review-ready rather than "rough notes."

This is the standard against which `Maddie`, `Rob`, and `Derek` deployments are measured today.
