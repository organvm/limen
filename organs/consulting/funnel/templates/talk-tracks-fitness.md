# Talk Tracks — Rob, BODi fitness lane

> Drafts only. Rob personalizes and types every send himself. Nothing here is
> a script to paste at volume — each track is a *shape* for one honest
> conversation with one person. `{{ROB_AFFILIATE_LINK}}` stays a slot until
> Rob provides his personal link (owed atom in his engagement record).

Tone reference: the 2026-04-25 call — brotherly, informal, direct. No hype,
no income claims, no pressure. Every track ends before it becomes a pitch the
person didn't ask for.

---

## Track 1 — Inbound reply (a comment or DM showed intent)

**When:** someone comments or DMs about a post — a question, a "this is me,"
a form-check ask.

**Shape:** answer the actual question first, fully, for free. Then, only if
they asked about training or their own routine:

> Appreciate you — that's exactly the wall I hit too. If you want, I put
> together free personalized plans for people, no strings — I just like
> seeing people get after it. Want me to send you the link?

**Disqualify gracefully:** if they need something Rob doesn't do (rehab,
medical, competitive prep):

> Honestly that's outside what I coach — you want someone who does
> [that thing] all day. I'd start with [pointer]. Rooting for you though.

## Track 2 — Opt-in invite (move a warm lead to the capture form)

**When:** they said yes to Track 1, or asked directly for help.

> Here you go: [capture form link]. Takes ~3 minutes — goals, schedule,
> what you've got access to. I read every one myself and build the plan
> around your actual week, not a template. Usually back to you within a
> couple days.

No follow-up chase if they don't fill it. One reminder max, a week later,
only if the conversation was warm:

> No pressure at all — form's still there if you want the plan. Either way,
> keep showing up.

## Track 3 — Plan delivery

**When:** form submitted, plan built.

> Here's your plan — built off what you gave me: [1-line personalization
> proof, e.g. "kept it to 3 days since your shifts are brutal"]. Two asks:
> tell me if anything feels off, and check in with me once a week — even
> just "done" or "skipped." That's the whole system. Want out of the
> check-ins anytime, just say so.

The check-in expectation is set *here*, at delivery — that consent is what
makes Track 4 warm instead of pushy.

## Track 4 — Check-in cadence (the warm loop)

**When:** weekly, per the Teamzy column. Celebrate first, troubleshoot
second, never pitch inside a check-in.

> Week [n] — how'd it go? [If they reported progress:] That's the compound
> interest right there. [If they struggled:] What got in the way — time,
> energy, or life? We adjust the plan, not the goal.

**The line that must not be crossed:** no offer language in a check-in. The
offer conversation (Track 5) opens only when the lead raises the buying
question ("what do you actually use?" / "is there a program?") **or** has
completed 2+ check-ins and Rob judges it right.

## Track 5 — Offer conversation (the one ask)

**When:** the entry condition above. Made personally, once.

> So — if you want to go past the free plan, here's what I actually run
> people on: [tier fit for them, from the ladder]. Straight up: that's my
> affiliate link ({{ROB_AFFILIATE_LINK}}), so BODi pays me a commission if
> you buy through it — costs you the same either way. If it's not the
> season for it, the free plan and the check-ins don't go anywhere.

**Respect the answer.** A "no" or a silence closes the track; the lead stays
in the warm loop at whatever cadence they consented to. No second ask unless
*they* reopen it.

## Track 6 — Ambassador invite (VIPs only, invitation only)

**When:** a VIP is *already* sharing Rob's content or sending friends,
unprompted. That behavior is the qualification — the invite just names it.

> You realize you've sent me like three people this month, right? If you
> want, you can get your own affiliate link so that actually pays you —
> same 1-level program I'm on, no team-building, no quotas, just your link.
> Happy to walk you through the signup if you're into it; zero sweat if not.

**Never:** income projections, "business opportunity" framing, recruiting
scripts, or inviting anyone who hasn't already demonstrated the behavior.

---

## The graceful exit (any track, any time)

> Totally get it — life's full. I'll stop the check-ins. If you ever want
> the plan dusted off, one message and we're back.

Dead leads get this note at the monthly prune, not silence. The exit is part
of the brand.
