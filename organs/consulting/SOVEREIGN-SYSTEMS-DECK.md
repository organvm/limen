## What this deck is

This is a **show-ready proof of the consulting macro-to-micro transformation**:

- The macro platform (`ORGANVM-as-a-service`) is the reusable body.
- The micro deployments are `Maddie`, `Rob`, and `Derek`.
- The beat is still manual and human-gated; the organ drafts and structures, the human sends, signs, and commits.

## The micro instances (current)

### 1) Maddie deployment

- **Role in the model:** tests scope capture under changing priorities.
- **What is proven:** engagement posture can be held even when requirements shift.
- **Current deliverables:** intake posture, assumptions log, delivery sequence, unresolved-item tracker.

### 2) Rob deployment

- **Role in the model:** tests recurring cadence and re-prioritization.
- **What is proven:** execution rhythm can stay stable when work packets are adjusted.
- **Current deliverables:** cadence tracker, milestone checkpoints, quality notes, next-step package.

### 3) Derek deployment

- **Role in the model:** tests portability across a different working idiom.
- **What is proven:** one operating model works without changing its private substrate terms.
- **Current deliverables:** structured brief format, milestone ledger, handoff package.

## The macro face this micro proves

The three deployments stress the same platform:

- explicit member/mandate/standing capture,
- versioned scope artifacts,
- no silent drift in commitments,
- and consistently reviewable outputs.

If any one thread is missing a clear gate or clean handoff, the system misses the bar. These proofs are where
that bar is checked today.

## Working constraints (repeated in this review)

- No autonomous outbound messages.
- No autonomous contract sends.
- No external commitments without human authorization.
- No irreversible action without explicit send/signature gate.
