# BBNC.net Modernization charter

**UNSIGNED WORKING DRAFT — NO BBNC AUTHORITY — NOT EFFECTIVE**

This document is completed only in a BBNC-authorized Phase 0 workshop. The executed revision belongs
in BBNC's private records system. This public draft carries no signatures or personal assignments.

## 1. Parties and authority

- **Institutional owner:** Bristol Bay Native Corporation (BBNC)
- **Proposed delivery party:** Padavano
- **Seed initiative:** BBNC.net Modernization
- **Working internal product name:** BBNC Stewardship, subject to BBNC brand approval

BBNC alone defines and delegates institutional authority. Padavano has no authority to interpret
BBNC policy, accept BBNC risk, approve an initiative, release content, represent BBNC publicly, or
bind BBNC. Padavano authority is limited to the exact engineering work package BBNC executes.

## 2. Purpose

Phase 1 discovers and baselines the authority, data, service, content, user-journey, accessibility,
performance, security, migration, and integration facts required to price and contract later work.
It does not authorize product implementation, production access, data migration, or public launch.

## 3. Product and system boundary

- BBNC Stewardship is proposed as the staff-only authority-to-outcome platform.
- BBNC.net is proposed as a separate public read model fed only by signed public packages.
- myBBNC and existing voting, employment, application, policy, compliance, and subsidiary systems
  remain systems of record.
- v1 platform storage is limited to Public and BBNC-approved Internal material.
- Confidential and Restricted material stays in its owner system behind an opaque reference,
  classification, owner, and fingerprint.

## 4. Explicit exclusions

Phase 1 excludes shareholder profiles, banking, enrollment documents, voting records, ethics reports,
investigations, customer-controlled data, authenticated portal replacement, production mutation,
public content changes, account creation, and any other activity not explicitly named in the executed
work package.

## 5. BBNC ownership

BBNC owns its data, content, brand, configuration, custom integrations, repositories, cloud accounts,
identity registrations, domains, DNS, keys, backups, logs, audit records, and delivery artifacts.
Pre-existing Padavano IP, if any, is scheduled separately. Any operating dependency needed for BBNC
to maintain its installation must be licensed or transferred in the executed agreement.

## 6. Required role assignments

BBNC records one opaque principal reference for each role in the private executed revision:

| Role | Decision authority |
|---|---|
| Executive sponsor | Program sponsorship, priority, escalation |
| Product owner | Requirements, backlog, user and operating acceptance |
| Legal/compliance owner | Applicability, contract, privacy, records, third-party controls |
| IT/security owner | Identity, infrastructure, security, incident, recovery |
| Records owner | Retention, legal hold, audit export, executed-charter custody |
| Communications owner | Public content, brand, publication, crisis coordination |
| Cultural-content steward | Cultural appropriateness and publication approval |
| Release authority | Exact release acceptance and rollback authority |

## 7. Acceptance and change control

Discovery, Stewardship Alpha, Stewardship production, website build, launch, and operations are
separately accepted work packages. Each package names scope, assumptions, price, dates, owners,
inputs, acceptance predicates, evidence, and change control. A later package is not authorized by
acceptance of an earlier one.

Changes bind to an exact revision. Material change invalidates affected approvals. Unresolved
critical/high findings block release. Disputed acceptance remains open until the named BBNC owner
records a decision or the parties follow the executed dispute process.

## 8. Access and security

Padavano access is named, least-privileged, time-bounded, logged, reviewable, and removable. BBNC
controls environment promotion, secrets, keys, privileged identities, backups, and audit exports.
No credential or private locator appears in the public prospect packet or authority receipt.

## 9. Phase 1 deliverables

The discovery package produces a BBNC-approved authority map, service blueprint, data map,
content/URL/media inventory, top journeys, baselines, risk register, and v1 requirements. The URL
ledger assigns every discovered legacy URL one terminal disposition.

## 10. Effective gate

This charter becomes effective only when the BBNC-owned executed revision is stored by the records
owner and `validate-phase0.py authorize` accepts its redacted authority receipt. The receipt grants
only the Phase 1 discovery work package named above.

## 11. Execution record — private BBNC copy only

The executed revision records exact revision ID, effective date, term, BBNC signatories, Padavano
signatory, role assignments, evidence references, and charter SHA-256. None of those fields are
completed in this public draft.
