## Why these three

These engagements were not chosen to be easy. They were chosen to stress the platform
across three different failure modes:

- **Maddie** — tests scope capture under shifting priorities (the most common consulting
  breakdown: the mandate keeps moving and no one writes it down).
- **Rob** — tests execution rhythm across recurring work packets (the second most common: the
  cadence holds until it doesn't, and no one tracks the delta).
- **Derek** — tests portability across a different working idiom (the platform assumption: the
  same process holds without being tuned to one personality).

If all three pass, the macro platform is proven portable.

## What the three deployments prove together

1. **Scope doesn't drift silently.** Every change is logged (Rob's scope change), or the
   unchanged scope is confirmed (Maddie and Derek's empty change logs). Silence is evidence.

2. **Posture is always named.** All three engagements are at EXECUTION. No engagement is
   "in progress somewhere" — the standing is an explicit state, not a feeling.

3. **Human gates are not advisory.** They are structural. Nothing moves to REVIEW without
   Anthony's sign-off. Nothing advances past REVIEW without his next gate.

4. **Portability holds.** The same five-primitive map, the same governance contract, and the
   same delivery posture sequence works for scope-stability (Maddie), cadence-stability (Rob),
   and cross-domain portability (Derek).

## Operating constraints (invariant across all three deployments)

These are not best practices. They are non-negotiable structural constraints:

- **No autonomous client-facing messages.** The organ drafts; the partner sends.
- **No autonomous contract sends or modifications.** Staged and surfaced only.
- **No autonomous billing.** Invoice drafts are reviewed before any send.
- **No external commitments without explicit gate.** The partner is the final authority.
- **No invented proof.** Deliverables and status reflect what actually exists in this repo
  and on human-approved channels. Placeholders are labeled as placeholders.
