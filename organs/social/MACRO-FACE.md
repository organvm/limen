## The problem this platform solves

The fundamental asymmetry of modern social life is **administrative**. The wealthy and
institutionally protected have a whole human infrastructure standing behind their relationships:
assistants who remember birthdays and send gifts, social secretaries who manage calendars and
correspondence, chiefs of staff who triage outreach and maintain networks, community operators
who hold institutions together, and alumni offices that keep ties alive across decades.

Everyone else holds the full weight of every relationship — every birthday, every debt, every
promise, every boundary, every overdue reply, every dormant friendship that needs care or needs
release — in one exhausted private mind.

The result is not just exhaustion. It is **relational collapse by entropy**: ties degrade because
the administrative burden of maintaining them exceeds capacity, not because the care isn't real.

---

## The thesis

> **To hold a community's worth of relationships is not a personality trait — it is an
> institutional function. The right infrastructure can carry the administrative weight so the
> human can carry the care.**

The platform runs on two synchronized engines:

| Engine | What it does | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **The Memory Engine** | Maintains a structured, consent-aware people registry with relationship-posture records: who each person is, what tie is held, where it stands (active · warm · dormant · strained · broken · protected), what reciprocity norm governs it, and what boundaries protect it | Relationships survive because the record of them survives — not because one person has a good memory. The engine remembers so the human can focus on the care, not the administrative overhead |
| **The Cadence Engine** | Tracks care obligations, overdue replies, important dates, repair opportunities, and boundary anniversaries. Stages drafts and reminders; never sends or acts without the human's review | Care is consistent because it is scheduled — not because the human happened to remember at the right moment. The engine surfaces what matters; the human does what is right |

Neither engine works without the other. Memory without cadence is a phone book. Cadence without
memory is an alarm with no context.

## What this platform is

A **structured relationship-operations toolkit** that runs the five functions a community
steward or personal chief of staff performs — stripped to their mechanical core, adaptable to
any relationship type and any community scale:

| Function | What it produces |
|---|---|
| **Registry** | A single source of truth for every person in the social field: identity, context, consent boundaries, shared history, and current relevance. Private by default; exposed only on a need-to-know basis |
| **Posture Record** | The full five-primitive relationship-posture brief: Member, Mandate, Standing, Standard, and Governance — one record per relationship, checkable and machine-readable |
| **Triage** | A sorted view of the relationship field by standing and urgency: who needs care, who needs a reply, who is dormant, who is in repair, who is protected. The daily or weekly relational dashboard |
| **Drafting** | Context-aware correspondence drafts and care actions staged for the human to review and send. The draft carries the relationship record so the human doesn't have to re-read the history |
| **Archive** | Relationship closeout or transition record: what the tie was, what care was given, why it changed, and any unresolved obligations. The record the next phase of the relationship starts from |

Each function is repeatable. Each feeds the next. The whole system can be held by one person
managing a personal network, a family, a mutual-aid group, a creative community, or a civic
institution.

## The five-primitive kernel

Every relationship is structured around five primitives. The same map works for any kind of
tie — family, friend, collaborator, student, client, neighbor, community member:

| Primitive | In social | Concretely |
|---|---|---|
| **Member** (person) | the person or group in the social field | identity, context, consent boundaries, shared history, and current relevance. Never reduced to contact info alone |
| **Mandate** (relationship) | the tie being held | what kind of relationship: friend, family, collaborator, student, client, neighbor, community member, or other named tie — and why it matters |
| **Standing** (tie strength) | where the relationship is right now | ACTIVE · WARM · DORMANT · STRAINED · BROKEN · PROTECTED — never "I should really reach out" (that is administrative noise, not standing) |
| **Standard** (reciprocity norm) | the expected pattern of care | expected cadence, reply window, boundaries, obligations, mutuality, and what good stewardship of this tie looks like |
| **Governance** (community rules) | who may see or act | what requires consent, what boundaries cannot be crossed, what stays irreducibly in the human's hand, and how conflicts are handled |

A relationship can only enter the system when each primitive is named. No floating contacts.
No drifting standing. No silent boundary changes.

---

## The posture sequence

```
ACTIVE → WARM → DORMANT → STRAINED → BROKEN → PROTECTED
```

Posture rules:
- Standing advances and regresses in response to real changes in the relationship — it is a
  diagnostic, not a judgment.
- Every standing change is logged explicitly with the reason and surfaced to the human.
- PROTECTED means the relationship has a boundary rule that blocks contact, sharing, or
  escalation — the organ honors it before any reminder, draft, or workflow.
- BROKEN means the tie has been severed, by either side, with an optional closeout record.
- Repairs move from BROKEN or STRAINED back toward ACTIVE, but only when the human confirms
  the repair action.

## What the operator actually receives

1. **A People Registry** — one record per person in the social field: member, mandates,
   standing, standard, governance, human gates, and change log. Private by default.
2. **A Relationship-Posture Brief** — a single-page summary of any tie: who, why, where it
   stands, what is expected, and what is sacred.
3. **A Triage Dashboard** — the current state of the relationship field sorted by what needs
   the human's attention: overdue care, unreplied messages, approaching dates, repair
   opportunities, and boundary conflicts.
4. **A Care Cadence Plan** — what the organ handles (reminders, drafts, standing checks) and
   what requires the human's high-leverage presence (the actual call, message, visit, repair).
5. **A Relationship Archive** — closeout or transition records for ties that have ended or
   changed form. The record the next phase starts from.

## What this is not

This platform does not manipulate relationships, send messages autonomously, surveil people, or
replace consent. It does not reduce people to scores, optimize relationships for extraction, or
appoint itself as a community authority.

It also does not practice therapy, counseling, or mediation. It is **relationship-stewardship
infrastructure**, not a therapeutic or social-work platform.

The constraint is a feature. It enforces the boundary that keeps the platform trustworthy and
the human's relational agency intact.

## Who holds this platform

The macro form of Koinonia is intentionally generic:

- No hardcoded person names
- No relationship-type-specific assumptions
- No idiom tuned to one culture, one community type, or one relational personality

A solo founder who wants to maintain a growing professional network without hiring a chief of
staff, a parent managing extended family obligations across three generations, a mutual-aid
organizer holding a community's people registry, or an artist maintaining a web of collaborators
and patrons — all four hold the same toolkit and fill in their own Members, Mandates, and
Standards.

The proof that it holds across different relationship types, different scales, and different
relational contexts is the micro instance.

## Governance layer (the authority contract, plainly stated)

The organ runs the social infrastructure. The human runs the relationships.

| What the organ does | What the human does |
|---|---|
| Maintains the people registry and posture records | Confirms accuracy; corrects errors; revokes data |
| Ties the relationship field, surfaces standing changes | Confirms standing changes; decides next action |
| Drafts correspondence and care actions | Reviews and sends; never sent without review |
| Tracks cadence, flags overdue care and approaching dates | Adjusts cadence; cancels reminders when timing is wrong |
| Enforces boundaries before any workflow | Sets and revokes boundaries; confirms enforcement |
| Archives relationship records on closeout | Approves the closeout; keeps or destroys the record |

No autonomous outreach. No autonomous relationship changes. No shared data without consent.
The human is the final authority for every relational act.
