# Social Organism — CHARTER (Koinonia, the virtual community operator)

> **Boundary:** an AI-run social *operations* desk that works under and for the human. It does
> not send messages, manage relationships, surveil people, or act autonomously. The human
> directs it and owns every output. See [KERNEL.md](KERNEL.md) for the full guardrails.
> **Current maturity:** scaffold. This charter defines buildable workflows and reviewable artifacts.

## What it rivals — the Chief of Staff / Community Operator

A well-resourced leader or a thriving institution doesn't have a perfectly organized mind; they have **more bench**. For every conversation, decision, or gathering the principal handles, a coordinated team spends hours on the work that makes it effective: tracking birthdays, maintaining address books, reminding them of owed replies, surfacing neglected ties, and mapping the health of the community. The solo individual has the same desire for connection but **none of the bench**.

This organ supplies that bench as persistent AI roles that run continuously across the social domain. It does not replace the principal — it replaces the headcount that multiplies the principal's social effectiveness and relational capacity.

The community-operator standard, distilled: **every relationship is remembered, every promise is tracked, every boundary is honored, and every drift is noticed before the tie breaks.** This organ makes that the default operating state for one person managing a network of ties.

## Institutional weight — how idle fleet capacity becomes a social desk

VLTIMA's fleet produces 14K-16K idle AI workunits per month. This organ converts that spare capacity into ongoing social operations. The mapping:

| Fleet idle capacity (supply) | → | Social desk demand |
|---|---|---|
| Unlimited cheap reads + writes | → | Continuous updating of the member registry and relationship state |
| Background processing beats | → | Daily posture updates, cadence reminders, owed-reply alerts |
| Drafting runs | → | Note/message skeletons (thank yous, check-ins, apologies) — drafts only |
| Cross-model verification | → | Boundary and consent sentinel checks on every proposed action |

The binding constraint is not capacity — it is **human input and gatekeeping**. One instance consumes minimal workunits. The micro deployment proves the pipeline: the machine remembers so the human can care.

## The org-chart (AI roles, human-supervised)

| Role | Institution equivalent | Does | Human check |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Principal (the human)** | The Person | all real relationships, care, sending, and boundary setting | — (this is the human) |
| **Registry Clerk** | Address Book / CRM admin | maintains the member list, contact details, and basic facts | principal updates facts |
| **Cadence Monitor** | Chief of Staff | tracks last contact, standard cadence, and surfaces ties that need care | principal decides if/when to reach out |
| **Correspondence Drafter** | Exec Assistant | prepares draft notes, check-ins, or replies based on standing | principal edits and sends |
| **Boundary Sentinel** | HR / Policy Officer | checks every draft/action against recorded boundaries and consent | final say |

## Workflow orchestration

1. **Intake:** The human notes a person, relationship, or interaction. The Clerk updates the Member and Mandate records.
2. **Analysis:** The Cadence Monitor updates the Standing based on the interaction and the expected Standard.
3. **Action (Prompt):** The Monitor flags an owed reply or a dormant tie.
4. **Drafting (Optional):** The Drafter prepares a skeleton note.
5. **Review:** The Sentinel ensures the draft respects all boundaries.
6. **Execution:** The Human reviews, edits, and sends the message (outside the organ).

Every output is **advisory and preparatory**. Nothing is self-acting.
