# Social Organism — KERNEL (Koinonia)

> **Boundary (load-bearing, repeated everywhere in this organ):** this is social-institution
> *infrastructure* that AUGMENTS human care, memory, and judgment. It does **not** manipulate
> relationships, send messages autonomously, surveil people, or replace consent. Every outreach,
> invitation, reconciliation attempt, boundary, and community rule stays with the human who owns
> the relationship. The organ remembers, organizes, drafts, prompts, and routes so one person can
> hold a community's worth of relational weight without pretending the machine is the friend.

## Why this organ exists

People do not flourish through isolated productivity. They survive and grow through durable ties:
family, friends, collaborators, neighbors, patrons, teachers, students, communities, and the
ordinary correspondence that keeps those ties alive. The rich and institutionally protected have
assistants, chiefs of staff, clubs, churches, alumni offices, social calendars, and community
operators standing behind them. Everyone else is expected to remember every birthday, debt, promise,
boundary, introduction, conflict, and opportunity from an exhausted private mind.

This organ is the institutional prosthesis for social life. It gives one person, and then anyone, the
infrastructure of a civic/community institution plus relationship support: a living memory of people,
ties, obligations, norms, and rules, with the human still deciding what care, contact, repair, or
distance is right.

This organ was named by Anthony as **Koinonia**. It is intentionally unscoped at the substrate: not a
CRM, not a dating tool, not a social-media scheduler, and not a single community app.
Generic + nameless underneath; his instance on top.

## The 5-primitive kernel, mapped to the social domain

| Primitive | Social meaning | Concretely |
|---|---|---|
| **Member** | person / contact | the person or group in the social field: identity, context, consent boundaries, shared history, and current relevance |
| **Mandate** | relationship | the tie being held: friend, family, collaborator, student, client, neighbor, community member, or other named relationship |
| **Standing** | tie strength | where the relationship stands now: active, warm, dormant, strained, broken, protected, or requiring care |
| **Standard** | reciprocity norm | the expected pattern of care, reply, obligation, boundary, cadence, and mutuality for this kind of tie |
| **Governance** | community rules | who may see or act on relational information, what requires consent, what boundaries cannot be crossed, and how conflicts are handled |

This is the *same* kernel as the legal, governance, health, and artist organs — only the skin changes.
That is the fractal: one structure, every pillar.

## Fractal deployment

- **MACRO** — community + relationship infrastructure anyone can hold: a consent-aware people
  registry, relationship-mandate records, correspondence support, care reminders, boundary tracking,
  conflict/repair workflows, community rulebooks, and privacy-preserving memory. It is the social
  infrastructure a neighborhood, school, mutual-aid group, founder network, church, creative circle,
  or family system can adopt without turning people into sales leads.

- **MICRO** — Anthony's network, correspondence, and relationship support: a private instance that
  helps him remember who matters, what each tie needs, which messages need replies, where care is
  overdue, which boundaries are active, and where a relationship needs repair, gratitude, distance, or
  a drafted note for him to review and send.

The micro instance proves the macro platform. The macro platform keeps the substrate generic enough
that it can serve another person, community, or institution without carrying Anthony's private names
or assumptions inside the lower layer.

## First slice

The first usable slice is a **relationship-posture brief**:

1. A person/contact record that names the Member without exposing private details by default.
2. A relationship Mandate that states what tie is being held and why it matters.
3. A Standing field that captures tie strength and urgency without reducing a person to a score.
4. A Standard that records the reciprocity norm: expected cadence, boundaries, owed replies, and care.
5. A Governance note that says who may act, what requires Anthony's hand, and what must never be sent
   or shared automatically.

That brief becomes the seed for later workflows: correspondence triage, care cadence, introductions,
community moderation, repair planning, and boundary protection.

## Hard guardrails (every contributor + every dispatched task)

- No autonomous outreach. Messages, invitations, introductions, apologies, and boundary statements are
  drafted and staged; the human sends or does not send.
- No manipulation. The organ supports care, memory, reciprocity, and repair; it does not optimize
  people as funnels, pressure targets, parasocial assets, or status tokens.
- Consent and privacy are structural. Private relationship facts stay private, access is minimized,
  and community memory is held only for legitimate relational care.
- Tie strength is posture, not human worth. A relationship state guides attention; it is never a score
  of a person's value.
- Boundaries outrank cadence. If a person or community rule says no contact, no sharing, or no
  escalation, the organ preserves that boundary before any reminder, workflow, or growth goal.
- Community governance belongs to the community. The organ can draft rules, surface conflicts, and
  track decisions; it does not appoint itself as authority over real people.
