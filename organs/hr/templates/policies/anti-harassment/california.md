## Policy

[Company Name] is committed to maintaining a workplace free of discrimination,
harassment, retaliation, and bias. This policy applies to all employees,
contractors, interns, and applicants.

Prohibited conduct includes harassment based on race, color, religion, sex
(including pregnancy, childbirth, and related conditions), sexual orientation,
gender identity, gender expression, national origin, ancestry, age (40+),
disability, medical condition, genetic information, marital status, military
or veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual
favors, and other verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature.

## California-Specific Requirements

- California Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA) covers more protected
  classes than federal law and applies to employers with 5+ employees.
- AB 1825 requires 2 hours of sexual harassment prevention training for
  supervisors every 2 years.
- SB 1343 requires 1 hour of sexual harassment prevention training for
  non-supervisory employees (2 hours for supervisors) every 2 years (5+
  employees).
- California prohibits discrimination based on off-duty cannabis use
  (AB 2188, effective 2024).
- California requires employers to provide harassment prevention materials
  in the language spoken by employees.
- The Department of Fair Employment and Housing (DFEH) is the enforcement
  agency. Complaint procedures must be clearly communicated.

## Reporting Procedure

1. Report harassment or discrimination to [manager/HR/designated contact].
2. If the report involves the designated contact, report to [alternative].
3. All reports will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially.
4. Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is strictly prohibited.
