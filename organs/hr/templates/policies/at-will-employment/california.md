# At-Will Employment — California
*Draft — for practitioner review. Not binding until reviewed by counsel.*

> **Jurisdiction:** California
> **Mandatory:** Yes (all private employers)
> **Last updated:** [date]

## Policy

Employment with [Company Name] is at-will. This means that either you or the
company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without
cause or advance notice.

Nothing in this policy, any employee handbook, or any company communication
creates an employment contract or alters the at-will nature of employment.

## California-Specific Notes

- California Labor Code Section 2922 establishes the presumption of at-will
  employment.
- The at-will presumption can be overcome by an express or implied contract.
- Written employment agreements, offer letters, and handbook statements must
  not inadvertently create contractual rights.
- Implied contracts may arise from company practices, oral statements, or
  Progressive discipline policies.
- California courts interpret at-will narrowly; disclaimers must be clear,
  conspicuous, and consistently applied.

## Practitioner Notes

- Review the client's offer letters, employment agreements, and verbal
  representations for language that could create contractual rights.
- Ensure disclaimers appear in the handbook, offer letters, and any
  performance-related communications.
- *Practitioner: have employment counsel review this policy for the specific
  client context.*
