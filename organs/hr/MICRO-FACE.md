## The Jessica standing: what DISCOVERY means

DISCOVERY is the first posture in the engagement sequence. At this stage, the organ
is doing what intake does — recording who the practitioner is, what the mandate
states, what scope boundaries apply, and what evidence exists to inform the proposal.

**What happens at DISCOVERY:**
- Intake clerk (AI role) creates the structured posture record
- Practitioner (Jessica) has not yet confirmed scope or consented to downstream work
- No client-facing output is produced
- No Styx product recommendations are made

**What unlocks PROPOSAL:**
- Jessica confirms the engagement scope and consents to the posture record
- Anthony approves the proposal direction
- The first client candidate is identified

## Known constraints

- **No employment decisions or workplace counsel** — the organ is delivery infrastructure.
  Jessica makes all client decisions.
- **No outreach in Jessica's name** — the organ never communicates with clients,
  candidates, or third parties. Jessica owns all external communication.
- **No client commitments** — the engagement record is a draft. Nothing is binding
  until Jessica accepts a client and signs an engagement letter.
- **Styx behavioral tooling is consent-first** — no opt-in is assumed. Every Styx
  integration in a client deployment requires explicit consent from the team.

## Next proof step — from DISCOVERY to PROPOSAL

The first concrete artifact that proves the organ works for Jessica is:

1. **Jessica confirms the engagement scope** — she has spoken for herself and the
   posture record reflects her actual practice, not an assumption.
2. **First client candidate identified** — a real business that needs HR infrastructure
   (handbook, compliance calendar, or offboarding support).
3. **Proposal packet drafted** — W1 produces a client posture record for the candidate
   with scope, jurisdiction, and services. The proposal stays in draft for Jessica's
   review.
4. **Validator green** — both the consulting-org and HR-org validators pass against
   the engagement record.

Once Jessica accepts her first client and the organ produces its first deliverable
(handbook draft, compliance calendar, or offboarding checklist) that she reviews and
delivers, the HR organ has its first end-to-end proof.
