# HR Organism — CHARTER (the people office)

> Boundary: see `KERNEL.md`. Infrastructure augmenting human HR judgment; no
> employment decisions, no counsel, ever.

## What it rivals

A Fortune-500 people office fused with a boutique HR consultancy: the fixed
routines (hiring loops, onboarding, policy, compliance cadence, performance,
offboarding) that give large employers their institutional advantage —
rebuilt as artifacts one practitioner can wield for many small clients.

## The org-chart (AI roles, human-supervised)

| Role | Runs | Never |
|---|---|---|
| Intake clerk | client/role intake into structured posture records | commits scope |
| Policy drafter | handbook + policy drafts from the template library | issues policy |
| Compliance calendarist | jurisdiction-aware compliance calendar drafts | files anything |
| Performance framework keeper | rubric + review-cycle scaffolds (Styx-compatible) | evaluates a person |
| Offboarding steward | checklist + transition-doc drafts | executes a separation |

Every role stages drafts; the human practitioner (micro: Jessica) reviews,
owns, and delivers. The consulting organ's engagement rules (manual mode,
human gates, scope integrity) govern all client work.

## The workflows it runs

1. **Practice intake** — a new client becomes a posture record with mandate,
   standing, and standard named.
2. **Handbook assembly** — policy skeleton → tailored draft → practitioner
   review → client delivery.
3. **Compliance cadence** — recurring calendar of filings/renewals/reviews,
   surfaced as drafts before every deadline.
4. **Performance/habits limb** — Styx-backed accountability programs, consent
   -first, for teams that opt in.
5. **Offboarding** — checklist-driven, dignity-preserving, fully human-gated.

## Inputs / outputs

- **Consumes:** the consulting organ's engagement records; the Styx product
  constellation; the funnel engine's HR-niche instance
  (`organs/consulting/FUNNEL-ENGINE.md`).
- **Produces:** versioned practice templates (macro), client-ready drafts
  (micro), and the Styx product tie for the performance limb.
