- **Fitt 3:** The kingdom's exhaustion and Beowulf's summoning (the nadir)
- **Fitt 4:** The hero's journey across the sea

**The Task:** Create a four-fitt music arc that mirrors these narrative movements through thematic force, emotional progression, and scene-by-scene composition.

**The Approach:**

## Key Insights You Should Know

### 1. The Nadir Is Fitt 3, Scene 2
This is where the Danes reach their deepest despair (heathen sacrifice). The music should reach its darkest, most structurally broken moment here. Everything before leads to this; everything after emerges from it.

### 2. Grendel's Presence Extends Beyond Fitt 2
While Fitt 2 establishes Grendel, his occupation continues as an unspoken background through Fitts 3–5. The music should carry harmonic memory of Fitt 2's dissonance.

### 3. Law and Protocol Are Structural Values
Fitt 5's insistence on proper form (coast-guard challenge, Beowulf's courteous reply) is not decoration. The poem claims that order, even under mortal threat, is civilization's foundation. Your music should reflect law as beauty, not bureaucracy.

### 4. Transitions Between Fitts Are Crucial
- **Fitt 2→3:** From eruption to helplessness (the weight becomes clear)
- **Fitt 3→4:** From nadir to hope (the answer begins)
- **Fitt 4→5:** From passage to threshold (entry becomes protocol)

Plan how your tracks will bridge these moments.

### 5. Each Fitt Has a Different Musical Register
- **Fitt 2:** Dark, isolated, relentless
- **Fitt 3:** Exhausted, sparse, with moments of dissonance
- **Fitt 4:** Energetic, rhythmic, forward-moving
- **Fitt 5:** Formal, clear, ceremonial

Don't expect the same emotional texture across all four fitts.

## The Structure (Four Movements)

**Movement I (Fitt 2):** Grendel's Isolation and Eruption
- Dissonant isolation → crescendo into violence → sustained despair
- Length: ~25–30 minutes

**Movement II (Fitt 3):** The Kingdom's Exhaustion
- Dirge-like exhaustion → spiritual nadir → ascending hope (the turn)
- Length: ~25–30 minutes
- **Contains the emotional center of the entire arc**

**Movement III (Fitt 4):** The Hero's Embarkation and Passage
- Martial gathering → rhythmic seafaring → triumphant arrival
- Length: ~20–25 minutes

**Movement IV (Fitt 5):** Threshold and Recognition
- Formal challenge → clear declaration → welcoming transition
- Length: ~20–25 minutes

**Total Duration:** Approximately 90–110 minutes

## Next Steps

### Step 1: Read for Understanding
Read BEOWULF-FITTS-2-5-MUSIC-COMPOSITION-GUIDE.md to understand the thematic framework.

### Step 2: Identify Your Musical Register
Decide whether these fitts continue the Fitt I register (Barber, Shostakovich) or shift into a different compositional language.

### Step 3: Select Tracks
For each of the 12 scenes, consult FITTS-2-5-COMPOSER-GUIDANCE.md and select or compose a track that fits:
- The thematic force for that scene
- The emotional progression within the fitt
- The overall four-fitt arc

### Step 4: Check Continuity
Ensure your tracks flow naturally:
- Within each fitt (scenes connect smoothly)
- Across fitts (transitions are marked but purposeful)
- Across the four-fitt arc (emotional progression is clear)

### Step 5: Document Your Selections
Create a FITTS-2-5 equivalent of studium/music/beowulf/book-01.yaml. Record:
- Your track selections
- Reasoning for each choice
- Any deviations from suggested approaches
- Notes on how the arc works

### Step 6: Test & Iterate
Listen to the full arc (or read through it) alongside the poem. Does the music guide the listener through the same emotional progression as the poem? Refine as needed.
