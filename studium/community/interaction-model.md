# The Studium as a multi-medium seminar — interaction model

> *"all of this stuff really adds to building a community for interaction on many different mediums —
> and as a professor uses their class as a breeding zone and war zone for their ideas re: their most
> new tbr book — we create systems of interaction for studying and learning and trial and packaging as
> data analysis."*

The Studium is not a reading list with a chat attached. It is a **seminar across mediums**: the same
passage is encountered five ways — read, copied by hand, translated, scored to music, screened against
a film — and the *interaction between people across those mediums* is the thing being built. A
professor runs a class as a **lab** (breed the idea) and an **arena** (battle-test it) for the book
they are writing. This model makes the Studium that, in public, with every interaction captured as
analyzable data.

## The mediums → surfaces
Each commentary system is also a way to interact, and each maps to a surface (Discord channel) in
`discord-plan.md`. No single medium is the point; the **crossing** between them is.

| Medium (commentary system) | Interaction | Surface |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Reading** — the encounter | where we are in the canon; the day's main question | `#the-reading` |
| **Handwriting** — the third system | share the day's hand-copied script / daily-page | `#scriptorium` |
| **Translation** — the encounter made labor | compare translations; argue a word | `#the-forum` (per-work threads) |
| **Music** — the second system | the force-matched arc; the weekly listening | `#listening-room` (voice) |
| **Film** — the fourth system | the force-matched film; the weekly Watch-Along | `#screening-room` |
| **Trial** — the war zone | stage / defend / attack a claim about a passage | `#trials` |

## The two engines under it
- **The breeding zone (lab).** A claim is incubated: a daily note becomes a thesis, cross-pollinated by
  the other mediums (the score heard a tension the translation missed; the film made a structure legible).
  The synthesis layer (`../synthesis/concepts.yaml`) is the field of claims; `current-inquiry.md` names
  the season's live one.
- **The war zone (arena).** A claim is stress-tested against the canon's **own** opposition — the
  counter-systems (`../synthesis/counter-systems.md`): the Gita against Achilles, the Dao against fatum.
  `trial-protocol.md` is the format; `../rubric/seminar.md` scores the conduct; the outcome is logged.

## How the mediums feed one conversation
The five readings of one force converge: a week might read Iliad 22 (the death of Hector), copy the
Greek, translate the supplication, listen to the funeral-march arc, screen *Letters from Iwo Jima*, and
then **try the claim** "the Iliad's mercy is structural, not sentimental" against the Heike's *mono no
aware*. That single week generates a stack of interaction events — rubric scores, a KEEP/REPLACE on the
arc, a film reaction, a trial round — which `../analysis/` packages into data: *which concepts the
community contests most, which claims keep surviving, where the counter-systems bite hardest.* That
data is the professor's notebook for the book.

## Posture (his gates)
Everything here is **structure, staged**. Provisioning the server, posting, inviting, and **collecting
any data from real people** are his levers — consent-gated, never automatic ([[known-owned-pervasive-then-idgaf]],
[[no-never-happens-again]]). The interaction model and the data schema exist so that *when* he opens the
gate, the seminar already knows how to run and how to learn from itself. Until then the only live data
is his own solo `../ledger/` (see `../analysis/PLAN.md`).
