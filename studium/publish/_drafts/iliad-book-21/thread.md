🧵 Iliad Book XXI: River, Fire, the Gods at War

Scoring this not as "war" but as its real emotional arc — wrath → wrath → divine-intervention → fate → supplication. One piece per beat. 👇

— — —

1/ Achilles drives Trojans into the Scamander — mass slaughter, the river choked with corpses
🎵 Shostakovich — Symphony No. 8 — II. Allegretto
The opening scene is mechanical killing at industrial scale — Achilles at the river is not heroic combat, it is massacre. Shostakovich's Eighth is the great symphony of inhuman violence: the Allegrett

— — —

2/ Lycaon's supplication — Achilles refuses mercy, kills Priam's son at the water's edge
🎵 Bach — St. Matthew Passion — 'Erbarme dich, mein Gott'
Lycaon falls at Achilles' knees: the supplication posture, the appeal to shared humanity, the plea that Achilles remember his own mortality. Achilles refuses — coldly, philosophically, without anger. 

— — —

3/ The Scamander rises in fury — the river-god becomes a divine force opposing Achilles
🎵 Sibelius — The Oceanides, Op. 73
The Scamander's awakening is not a flood — it is the natural world reconstituting itself as will. Sibelius' "The Oceanides" is water-music that is genuinely mythic: not decorative wave-painting but so

5/ Achilles prays to Zeus — supplication inside near-death, the cry of a man who faces his fate
🎵 Pärt — Spiegel im Spiegel
At the height of the flood Achilles calls on Zeus — not a warrior's prayer but a drowning man's. It is one of the rawest moments of vulnerability in the poem: the greatest fighter reduced to clinging

7/ The gods clash on the plain — Ares vs. Athena, Aphrodite felled, Poseidon vs. Apollo

The divine battle is simultaneously terrifying and absurd — gods striking gods, Aphrodite groaning on the ground, Hera boxing Artemis' ears. Stravinsky's "Danse sacrale" is the right register for this
