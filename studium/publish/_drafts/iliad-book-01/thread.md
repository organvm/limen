🧵 Iliad Book I: Rage, Plague, Olympus

Scoring this not as "war" but as its real emotional arc — wrath → plague → wrath → supplication → wrath. One piece per beat. 👇

— — —

2/ Greek sacrificial horizon / war-before-war
🎵 Gluck — Iphigénie en Aulide — Overture to Act I
The Achaean world is already morally contaminated by sacrifice, rank, command, forced exchange. Book I turns on a brutal grammar of exchange: Chryseis, Briseis, ransom, honor-prizes, plague as counter

— — —

3/ Trojan doom / Cassandra register
🎵 Berlioz — Les Troyens — 'Malheureux roi!'
Berlioz as mythic atmosphere — Cassandra's prophetic dread, doomed royal knowledge, not generic epic grandeur.

— — —

4/ Apollo's plague atmosphere
🎵 Berlioz — Les Troyens — Act II Introduction
Chryses' prayer is a successful appeal to divine order; Apollo enforces insulted sacred hierarchy. A world where human arrogance triggers a force above human law.

— — —

5/ Agamemnon vs. Achilles
🎵 Holst — The Planets — 'Mars, the Bringer of War'
The 5/4 pulse is hostile, asymmetrical, hard to settle into — a broken heroic economy of prizes, status, command, possession, visible honor. The scene is verbal, but already war.

— — —

6/ Quarrel as ritualized violence
🎵 Stravinsky — The Rite of Spring — 'The Augurs of Spring'
The ritual violence underneath heroic society. Holst gives military aggression; Stravinsky gives ritualized social violence — both levels of the quarrel.

— — —

7/ Athena restrains Achilles
🎵 Brahms — Symphony No. 4 — I. Allegro non troppo
Achilles grips the sword and does not draw it. Brahms is form under pressure, rage under grammar — Athena redirects wrath from murder into speech, then withdrawal. The poem is haunted by the murder Ac

— — —

8/ Achilles withdraws to the shore
🎵 Debussy — La mer — 'De l'aube à midi sur la mer'
After the masculine assembly, Achilles returns to the shore and calls Thetis. The sea is maternal, pre-human, divine, liminal — a living threshold.

— — —

9/ Achilles and Thetis
🎵 Mahler — Symphony No. 5 — IV. Adagietto
The funeral march belongs later (Patroclus, Hector, Book XXIV). In Book I, Achilles is insulted, wounded, alive, loved by his mother. The Adagietto is private, suspended, self-enclosed grief — wounded

— — —

10/ Thetis before Zeus / cosmic machinery
🎵 Wagner — Das Rheingold — Prelude
Grows like a world-system coming into being — Thetis converts Achilles' private injury into cosmic policy. Her appeal is geopolitical; Zeus' nod becomes a mechanism that will kill men.

— — —

11/ Olympus: divine authority
🎵 Handel — Music for the Royal Fireworks — Overture
Ceremonial authority, court splendor, sovereign display — Zeus' world, power staged as grandeur. Olympus needs court music: rank, order, display, rule.

— — —

12/ Olympus: gods as ridiculous
🎵 Rameau — Platée — Ouverture
The Olympian ending is grand and ridiculous at once: Zeus and Hera quarrel; Hephaestus mediates; the gods laugh; mortals suffer below. Book I ends in divine comedy.

— — —

13/ Olympus: farcical immortal theater
🎵 Offenbach — Orpheus in the Underworld — Overture
Only at the end, after Hephaestus restores laughter — it exposes the scandal of the Olympians: immortals laugh, drink, flirt, quarrel while humans are arranged for death.
