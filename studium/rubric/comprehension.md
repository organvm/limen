# Rubric — Comprehension

Comprehension here means one thing: did the dominant force of the passage move through you, or did the text stay an object on the page. This rubric is **diagnostic, never gatekeeping** — a 1-day is not a failure, it is data about where the passage resisted and what to return to.

---

**What to score:** After completing the daily page, ask — *can I name what moved in this scene, and say why it moved?* That is the whole question.

---

## Level 1 — Nascent

The scene was read but its animating force did not surface. The DOMINANT FORCE field on the daily page is left blank, filled with the rubric's own category words ("wrath," "exile") without explanation, or named but immediately dropped — no sentence connects it to the actual lines. The ONE INTERTEXT is either empty or a mechanical gesture ("this is like another hero story"). In conversation you could summarize the plot but not explain why the passage has the weight it does.

## Level 2 — Emerging

The dominant force is named and there is at least one concrete moment from the passage to point to — a particular line, image, or word that carries it. The explanation stays local: you can say *this moment is wrath* but not yet *why this civilization needed wrath to hold the weight it put here*. The ONE INTERTEXT begins to make contact rather than just observe parallelism. The formal observation (meter, syntax, epithet) is present but not yet connected to the force.

## Level 3 — Developing

The dominant force is named, grounded in specific lines, and placed inside a larger structure — you can say what the civilization is *doing* with this force, not just that it is present. The LITERAL TRANSLATION shows you working out where exactly a word commits the text to that force rather than another. The ONE INTERTEXT is genuine: the second text changes how you read the first. The ONE FORMAL OBSERVATION is tied to the force rather than filed separately.

## Level 4 — Fluent

The dominant force is so clearly landed that the daily page does interpretive work: the LITERARY TRANSLATION and the FORMAL OBSERVATION reinforce each other, the intertext opens a question the passage alone could not have raised, and the WHY THIS COMPOSITION FITS answer names the same force in a different medium. You could hand someone the passage cold and point to the exact lines where the civilization bet everything on this particular encoding of violence, memory, law, or grief — and say what would be lost if those lines had chosen differently.

---

*Score once per day session, after completing the page. If the score is 1 or 2, note the specific sentence or line that stayed opaque — that is tomorrow's entry point, not a reason to re-read the whole scene.*
