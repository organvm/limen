## Quick-Reference Summary

### EXODUS: The Ascending Arc
- **Question:** Who is God?
- **Theological Work:** Covenant Establishment
- **Dominant Register:** Awe ascending to binding
- **Musical Movement:** Ascending + Luminous (revelation → awe → binding)
- **Force Arc:** revelation → divine-intervention → liberation → revelation → law → divine-intervention → law
- **Key Scenes:** Bondage/Calling → Plagues → Passover/Sea → Wilderness wonder → Sinai arrival → Covenant ratification → Breach/Repair → Tabernacle

---

### LEVITICUS: The Ritual Cycle
- **Question:** How shall we stay near God?
- **Theological Work:** Sacred Maintenance
- **Dominant Register:** Dread contained by sacred order
- **Musical Movement:** Ritually Contained (cycles, repetition, bounded access)
- **Force Arc:** law → divine-intervention → law → law → law
- **Key Scenes:** Offerings established → Priest consecration → Death/consequence → Purity cartography → Day of Atonement → Annual reset

---

### NUMBERS: The Descending Arc
- **Question:** What happens when we refuse?
- **Theological Work:** Generational Judgment
- **Dominant Register:** Tragedy and consequence
- **Musical Movement:** Descending + Judicial (attrition, slow loss, no arrival)
- **Force Arc:** law/judgment → divine-intervention → refusal → divine-intervention/judgment → judgment (sustained) → law/new order
- **Key Scenes:** First census → Wilderness provision → Spy mission → Refusal/sentence → Wilderness wandering → Moses excluded → Second census

---

### DEUTERONOMY: The Hortatory Plateau
- **Question:** Will we choose this?
- **Theological Work:** Covenant Renewal
- **Dominant Register:** Urgency and pleading toward choice
- **Musical Movement:** Hortatory + Urgent (persuasion, voice, pressure toward decision)
- **Force Arc:** revelation → covenant-renewal → exhortation → judgment/choice → choice threshold
- **Key Scenes:** Historical prologue → Call to new generation → Decalogue repreached → Love command → Laws with rationale → Blessing/curse → Choice set → Moses' farewell

## Cross-Book Architecture

The four books form a unified **theological symphony**:

| Book | Movement | Compositional Logic | Aesthetic |
|------|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Exodus | I | Ascent | Luminous wonder ascending into binding |
| Leviticus | II | Cycle | Dread held by ritual repetition |
| Numbers | III | Descent | Tragedy of consequence slowly executed |
| Deuteronomy | IV | Persuasion | Love-choice under urgent pressure |

**Critical principle:** Each book must sound **fundamentally different**. Do not allow compositional material to repeat across books. The unity comes from covenant-as-concept, not from musical similarity.

---

## Recommended Composition Sequence

1. **Exodus** — Start here. Ascending arcs are compositionally clear. The turning points are distinct. Establish this as your baseline.

2. **Leviticus** — Compose second. Ritual cycles will be understood better by contrast with Exodus' ascent. This book is compositionally unique and will discipline your approach.

3. **Numbers** — Compose third. The descending arc needs the contrast of Exodus (ascending) and Leviticus (cycling) to be clearly heard. Sustained attrition is hardest to sustain musically.

4. **Deuteronomy** — Compose last. Voice-dependent and narrative-driven. By this point, you have three distinct musical worlds established, and Deuteronomy's persuasive register will be clear.

Then **orchestrate and integrate** all four books into a unified statement.

## Key Compositional Principles

1. **No cross-book repetition.** Exodus should never sound like Leviticus. Numbers should never sound like Deuteronomy.

2. **Each book answers a different question.** Compose the answer to that question, not an variation on a theme.

3. **The force arc is not optional.** It is the skeleton of the composition. Make it audible.

4. **Register must shift at hinge points.** When Numbers transitions from judicial pronouncement (14:26) to wilderness attrition (15+), the register must shift. Not just vary—genuinely shift.

5. **The unified arc is audible only in retrospect.** Listeners will not know they are hearing a four-movement symphony until all four books are complete. Make that discovery rewarding.
