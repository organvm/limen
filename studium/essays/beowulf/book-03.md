# Beowulf Fitt III: The Arrival at Heorot
## A Music Arc for the Third Division

Fitt III moves from the beach to the golden hall. The dominant texture is formal procedure and architectural ascent. The Geats are permitted to pass, they march inland with boar-crests gleaming, and they finally stand outside Heorot to be questioned by Wulfgar. This is the poem at its most diplomatic and magnificent, demonstrating that even in a monster-haunted world, civilization maintains its rites.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Elgar — Symphony No. 1, I. Andante. Nobilmente e semplice**
Beowulf speaks his first words in the poem, identifying himself by his father's fame (Ecgtheow) and his lord (Hygelac). It is a speech of immense confidence and transparent nobility. Elgar's 'Nobilmente' captures this exact tone: a vast, unhurried statement of character that requires no aggression because its authority is complete.

**2. Holst — *The Planets*, Jupiter, the Bringer of Jollity**
The coastguard grants them passage, and they march up the stone-paved road. The poem dwells on their gleaming armor and the gold-adorned boar-figures on their helmets. Holst's 'Jupiter' possesses the right striding, expansive momentum for this march—it is the sound of strong men moving forward in the sunlight, destiny fully in their hands.

**3. Messiaen — *L'Ascension*, III. Alléluias sereins d'une âme qui désire le ciel**
They see the timbered hall, gleaming with gold. The poem pauses to admire Heorot's scale and brilliance. Messiaen provides the required sense of luminous, almost overwhelming verticality. It is not just a building; it is a vision of human achievement towering over the landscape.

**4. Bach — *The Art of Fugue*, Contrapunctus I**
Arriving at the door, the sea-weary men stack their shields and spears. They cannot enter armed. This is the strict architecture of peace and custom. Bach's fugue represents this perfect, unbending formal clarity—the rules of the house must be observed, even by heroes.

**5. Handel — *Zadok the Priest***
Wulfgar, a proud Vandal prince and Hrothgar's herald, steps forward to demand their names again. The bureaucracy of the court is functioning. Handel's coronation anthem, with its structured buildup, fits the high-court formality of the encounter. Wulfgar is the voice of the throne, demanding respect.

**6. Beethoven — Symphony No. 7, II. Allegretto**
Wulfgar turns away to deliver Beowulf's request to the old, gray-haired king sitting among his earls. The outcome hangs in the balance. Beethoven's Allegretto provides a walking, measured, fateful pulse—the inevitable approach of the messenger toward the center of power, carrying the news that could change everything.

---

## The Dominant Arc

The arc of Fitt III moves: **kingship → ascent → revelation → law → law → fate**. The dominant force is kingship, expressed through the mutual respect and rigid protocol of the warrior aristocracy. The music ascends through Holst and Messiaen before locking into the legal frameworks of Bach and Handel, ending on Beethoven's fateful pulse.
