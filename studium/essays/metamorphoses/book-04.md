# Metamorphoses Book IV: Weavers, Lovers, Monsters

Book IV concerns the boundaries of the permitted and the spaces where transformations occur in secret. The daughters of Minyas stay indoors weaving and telling stories; Perseus flies across the ends of the earth fighting sea monsters. The dominant force is memory—the act of storytelling itself, as the weavers recall old tragedies while inadvertently sealing their own doom.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Bach — *The Art of Fugue — Contrapunctus I*** — *law*

While the city worships Bacchus, the daughters of Minyas remain inside, devoted to Minerva, weaving wool and following the rules of their craft. Law requires formal clarity and architecture—the rigorous, interlocking logic of the loom.

**2. Elgar — *Enigma Variations — 'Nimrod'*** — *grief*

A tale of two lovers whispering through a crack in a wall, ending in a double suicide under a mulberry tree, staining its berries red forever. Grief requires a slow tempo and an inward line that builds to a tragic nobility.

**3. Mozart — *Così fan tutte — Overture*** — *comedy*

Vulcan forges an invisible net and catches his wife Venus in bed with Mars, inviting all the gods to laugh at them. Comedy requires dance rhythm and courtly exaggeration—the brilliant, cynical farce of Olympian adultery.

**4. Bach — *Matthäus-Passion — 'Erbarme dich'*** — *supplication*

Abandoned by the Sun god, Clytie sits on the bare ground for nine days, doing nothing but watching the sun move across the sky until she becomes a sunflower. Supplication requires a suspended cadence and an agonizingly exposed line of devotion.

**5. Schoenberg — *Verklärte Nacht*** — *metamorphosis*

The nymph Salmacis pulls the boy Hermaphroditus into her pool and prays they may never be separated. Their bodies melt together into a single, dual-sexed form. Fluid, chromatic harmony captures the unsettling dissolution of boundaries.

**6. Strauss — *Elektra — Agamemnon motif*** — *divine-intervention*

Bacchus finally punishes the weavers. Their looms turn to vines, the room fills with phantom beasts, and the sisters shrink into squeaking bats. A sudden, terrifying color shift and heavy brass represent the god's vengeance crashing in.

**7. Berlioz — *Symphonie fantastique — V. 'Dream of a Witches' Sabbath'*** — *wrath*

Juno descends to the underworld and hires the Fury Tisiphone to drive Ino and Athamas mad. The fury arrives with poison and snakes. Rhythmic pressure and grotesque orchestral colors capture the infected, spiraling horror of insanity.

**8. Tchaikovsky — *Symphony No. 6 ('Pathétique') — IV. Finale*** — *memory*

Old and broken by their family's tragedies, Cadmus and Harmonia leave Thebes and are transformed into gentle serpents, remembering the humans they once were. The faded recapitulation of grief marks the end of their human timeline.

**9. Strauss — *Ein Heldenleben — 'The Hero'*** — *ascent*

With Medusa's head in a bag, Perseus flies on winged sandals, casting his shadow on the earth below. Ascent requires a rising sequence and luminous orchestration, the sheer swagger of a hero moving through the sky.

**10. Messiaen — *Quatuor pour la fin du temps — VIII. Louange à l'Immortalité de Jésus*** — *revelation*

Perseus slays the sea monster, saves Andromeda, and places Medusa's head face-down in seaweed. The weeds instantly harden into coral. Stillness and verticality capture the quiet, magical hardening of the ocean's first reef.

---

## The dominant arc of Book IV

```
law → grief → comedy → supplication → metamorphosis → divine-intervention →
wrath → memory → ascent → revelation
```

Book IV argues that **memory** is the fundamental driver of these transformations. Each event traces this force through different domains, showing how mortal limits break under its pressure.
