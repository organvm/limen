# Metamorphoses Book III: Thebes, Dogs, Reflections

Book III is the tragedy of looking. Actaeon sees Diana naked; Semele demands to see Jupiter in his true form; Narcissus sees himself; Pentheus spies on the Bacchic rites. Every instance of unpermitted sight is punished by a total dissolution of the body. The dominant force is fate—the inexorable doom that hangs over the house of Cadmus from its founding.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Verdi — *La forza del destino — Overture*** — *fate*

Cadmus slays the serpent of Mars and is told he too will one day be a serpent. The house of Thebes is founded under a curse. Fate requires an inexorable motif, the sound of a destiny that cannot be outrun.

**2. Prokofiev — *Romeo and Juliet — 'Dance of the Knights'*** — *wrath*

Cadmus sows the dragon's teeth, and armed men sprout from the soil, instantly turning on each other in a frenzy of slaughter until only five remain. The rhythmic pressure and heavy orchestration capture an immediate, violent tribalism.

**3. Debussy — *Jeux*** — *metamorphosis*

Actaeon wanders away from the hunt and accidentally sees the goddess bathing. She splashes water in his face, and antlers begin to branch from his head. Fluid harmony and shifting orchestral color capture the sudden, silent rewriting of his body.

**4. Stravinsky — *The Rite of Spring — 'Sacrificial Dance'*** — *sacrifice*

Unable to speak, Actaeon is hunted and torn to pieces by his own dogs while his friends cheer them on. Sacrifice requires ritual weight and an immolation build—the brutal, collective destruction of a victim.

**5. Wagner — *Tristan und Isolde — 'Liebestod'*** — *desire*

Tricked by Juno, Semele makes Jupiter swear on the Styx to grant her a wish, then asks him to make love to her in his full divine splendor. It is a suicide by desire, requiring chromatic longing and an ecstatic, fatal climax.

**6. Messiaen — *Turangalîla-Symphonie — V. Joie du sang des étoiles*** — *divine-intervention*

Jupiter arrives in his lightning. Semele's mortal frame cannot withstand the presence of a god, and she is reduced to ashes. The sudden color shifts and overwhelming brass/piano contrast of Messiaen capture a cosmic, lethal radiance.

**7. Pärt — *Cantus in Memoriam Benjamin Britten*** — *revelation*

Tiresias, having lived as both man and woman, is blinded by Juno but given prophecy by Jupiter. He sees what others cannot. Revelation requires stillness, chanting strings, and a descending verticality that feels like pure sight.

**8. Fauré — *Pavane*** — *memory*

Narcissus falls in love with his own reflection, unable to grasp it, slowly wasting away on the bank. Memory requires nostalgic recall and faded recapitulation—a delicate, trapped circling around an image that is already lost.

**9. Vaughan Williams — *The Lark Ascending*** — *exile*

Rejected by Narcissus, Echo hides in caves until her body wastes away and only her voice remains. Exile requires wandering tonality and an exposed, solitary line drifting upward into nothingness.

**10. Beethoven — *Symphony No. 5 — I. Allegro con brio*** — *fate*

Pentheus mocks the rites of Bacchus and goes to the mountain to spy on them. He is spotted by his own mother, Agave, who in her madness sees him as a boar and tears off his head. The hammered insistence of fate closes the book on Thebes.

---

## The dominant arc of Book III

```
fate → wrath → metamorphosis → sacrifice → desire → divine-intervention →
revelation → memory → exile → fate
```

Book III argues that **fate** is the fundamental driver of these transformations. Each event traces this force through different domains, showing how mortal limits break under its pressure.
