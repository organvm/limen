# Inferno, Canto 17: The Usurers and the Summoning of Geryon

Canto 17 is the hinge between the violent circle and fraud. Dante sees three registers of
sin in one frame and then watches reason itself recruit a counterfeit instrument to continue
the descent. The canto is legal, theatrical, and terrifying.

Blasphemers burn in exposed pain, sodomites circle in flame and shame, usurers clutch
symbolic signs of the same attachment that ruled their lives above. Virgil intervenes to
maintain momentum and summons Geryon as the beast that moves them past the boundary.
This is the first explicit lesson that not all evil is loud, frontal violence; some evil
is procedural and deceptively beautiful.

## The Dominant Arc

The force_arc — `law, wrath, revelation, fate, law, metamorphosis` — is the canto's spine:

1. **Law** governs the transition to a new technical phase of descent.
2. **Wrath** marks the still-unmasked violence of blasphemy.
3. **Revelation** makes Geryon visible as a theological and structural anomaly.
4. **Fate** appears in the patterned suffering of usury.
5. **Law** returns as explicit naming and accounting.
6. **Metamorphosis** completes the handoff to the next canto's world.

## Dominant Force: Law

This canto is where Hell's jurisprudence becomes operational. The canto's core is not
mercy or fury alone, but the procedure that translates moral categories into transport.
