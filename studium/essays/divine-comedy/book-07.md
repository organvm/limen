# Inferno, Canto VII: Circle of Avarice & Wrath

Canto VII is the canto of circular punishment. It introduces a new structure to Inferno: the spectacle of sin eternally re-enacting itself, two opposing forces locked together in a machine that neither can escape. The hoarders and wasters eternally push great weights, eternally calling curses upon the opposite force. The wrathful fight on the surface of the Stygian Marsh; the sullen drown beneath it. The entire circle is a structure of futility: motion without progress, conflict without resolution.

The dominant force is not merely wrath but mechanical repetition—the way sin, when crystallized into punishment, reveals itself as a kind of grinding automation. The souls do not suffer because they are tortured by external forces; they suffer because their own vice has become their eternal condition.

## Dominant Force: Wrath

Wrath in Canto VII is not passionate anger but mechanical rage—the way anger, when made eternal and crystallized into punishment, becomes indistinguishable from the machinery of judgment. The hoarders and wasters curse each other endlessly; the wrathful beat each other in perpetual combat; the sullen have swallowed their rage inward until it drowns them. All are trapped in cycles that repeat infinitely. The music must register this mechanical horror: not the drama of conflict but the grinding, relentless return of the same gesture.
