# Music arcs — The Divine Comedy — Inferno, Canto 4

**Playlist title:** *Inferno, Canto IV: Limbo*
**Length:** about **60 minutes**
**Principle:** Limbo is characterized by 'desire without hope.' It is not a place of torment, but of an immense, dignified sorrow. The music must reflect the noble melancholy of the virtuous pagans.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Verdi — *La forza del destino — Overture* — Waking on the brink**

Dante wakes to thunder and finds himself on the edge of the abyss. The hammered insistence of Verdi's opening establishes the absolute reality of his descent into the underworld.

**2. Mahler — *Symphony No. 5 — Adagietto* — The sighs of the unbaptized**

Limbo echoes with sighs, not wails. The slow, inward line of Mahler's Adagietto captures the vast, quiet sorrow of those who are suspended forever in unfulfilled longing.

**3. Bach — *The Art of Fugue — Contrapunctus 1* — The noble castle**

The castle of the great pagans is an island of light and reason in the dark. Bach's formal clarity and perfect architecture represent the highest achievements of human intellect without divine grace.

**4. Elgar — *Enigma Variations — 'Nimrod'* — The great poets**

Homer, Horace, Ovid, and Lucan welcome Virgil back. Elgar's nostalgic, expansive recall perfectly scores this meeting of minds, full of dignity and the shared memory of greatness.

**5. Tavener — *The Protecting Veil* — The philosophers**

Aristotle, Socrates, and Plato sit in the green enamel. Tavener's stillness and verticality reflect the contemplation of truth, the closest the pagan mind can come to heavenly light.

**6. Vaughan Williams — *Fantasia on a Theme by Thomas Tallis* — Departure into darkness**

Dante and Virgil leave the light of the castle and return to the dark, wandering path. The open intervals and ancient, haunting tonality signify their exit back into the true exile of Hell.

---

## The Dominant Arc

```
fate → grief → law → memory → revelation → exile
```

Grief is the dominant force of this Canto.
