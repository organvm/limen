# Music arcs — The Divine Comedy — Inferno, Canto 3

**Playlist title:** *Inferno, Canto III: The Gate and the Neutrals*
**Length:** about **60 minutes**
**Principle:** Canto III is the shock of the first sensory encounter with Hell. It moves from the terror of the Gate's inscription to the pathetic horror of the Neutrals, culminating in the sublime dread of Charon and the crossing of Acheron.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Beethoven — *Symphony No. 5 — II. Andante con moto* — The Gate of Hell**

The inscription 'Abandon all hope, ye who enter here' is not angry; it is an eternal, inexorable decree. Beethoven's solemn procession captures the weight of this permanence.

**2. Sibelius — *Valse Triste* — The sighs of the uncommitted**

The Neutrals chose nothing, and so they are given nothing. The wandering, mournful tonality of Valse Triste fits their pathetic, aimless rushing after a blank banner.

**3. Holst — *The Planets — Mars, the Bringer of War* — The wasps and the worms**

They are stung eternally by wasps and hornets, weeping tears mixed with blood. The rhythmic, unrelenting pressure of Mars embodies their endless, meaningless torment.

**4. Stravinsky — *Symphony of Psalms — I. Exaudi orationem meam, Domine* — Charon the ferryman**

Charon enforces the boundary of Hell. He is institutional, not personal. Stravinsky's rigid, monumental architecture conveys the uncompromising rule of divine justice that Charon enacts.

**5. Wagner — *Tristan und Isolde — Prelude* — The souls gathering on the shore**

Divine justice spurs the damned so that fear becomes desire; they are eager to cross. Wagner's chromatic longing captures the tragic paradox of their compulsion toward their own ruin.

**6. Scriabin — *The Poem of Ecstasy* — The earthquake and the swoon**

The earth shakes, a red light flashes, and Dante faints. The overwhelming sensory flood and changing orchestral color of Scriabin perfectly match Dante's collapse into unconsciousness.

## The Dominant Arc

```
fate → exile → wrath → law → desire → metamorphosis
```

Law is the dominant force of this Canto.
