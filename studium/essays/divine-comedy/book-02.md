# Music arcs — The Divine Comedy — Inferno, Canto 2

**Playlist title:** *Inferno, Canto II: The Three Blessed Women*
**Length:** about **60 minutes**
**Principle:** Do not score Canto II as action, but as the internal drama of hesitation and the revelation of divine grace interceding in history.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Barber — *Adagio for Strings* — Dante's hesitation**

Dante falters, doubting his worthiness to undertake the journey. The slow, inward line of Barber's Adagio captures the heavy introspection and fear of the pilgrim.

**2. Stravinsky — *Octet* — Virgil's rebuke**

Virgil counters Dante's fear with reason and structure. Stravinsky's neoclassical clarity represents the firmness of classical reason restoring order to Dante's panicked mind.

**3. Messiaen — *L'Ascension — I. Majesté du Christ demandant sa gloire à son Père* — Beatrice's descent to Limbo**

Beatrice leaves Heaven to enter Limbo. The sudden color shift and verticality of Messiaen's chords announce a power from outside the system of Hell intervening.

**4. Pärt — *Cantus in Memoriam Benjamin Britten* — Beatrice's plea to Virgil**

Beatrice asks Virgil to help Dante out of love. The descending bell-like quality of Pärt's work perfectly scores the gentle but inexorable descent of grace.

**5. Bach — *Mass in B minor — 'Crucifixus'* — The Virgin Mary and Lucia**

The chain of grace originates with Mary and Lucia. The resolving radiance of Bach's choral work reflects the ultimate, unquestionable salvation that initiates the entire journey.

**6. Strauss — *Metamorphosen* — Dante's courage renewed**

Like a flower opening to the sun, Dante's will is restored. The fluid harmony of Strauss captures this internal, psychological transformation from paralysis to readiness.

**7. Tchaikovsky — *Symphony No. 4 — II. Andantino in modo di canzona* — Entering the deep wood**

The journey begins in earnest. The inevitable tread of fate, but quieter and more resolute now, carries them forward into the darkness.

## The Dominant Arc

```
grief → law → divine-intervention → supplication → salvation → metamorphosis → fate
```

Divine-intervention is the dominant force of this Canto.
