# Inferno, Canto VI: Circle of Gluttony

Canto VI marks a shift in Inferno's register. The circles so far have contained the most sympathetic of the damned—the lustful were beautiful and articulate; Limbo held the virtuous unbaptized. Canto VI is the first where the damned are rendered contemptible, reduced to appetite, their humanity erased by the very indulgence that was their sin. The gluttons are not tragic; they are degraded.

The circle of gluttony is a region of perpetual rain and filth. The souls lie in mire, swollen, weeping, complaining of the stench even as they lie in its source. Cerberus, the three-headed dog of mythology, guards them, barking, threatening, embodying appetite made monstrous. This is the canto's revelation: that gluttony—the refusal of measure, the elevation of bodily indulgence above all other goods—reduces the soul to the level of the beast.

## The Dominant Arc

The force_arc — `fate, wrath, plague, desire, wrath, metamorphosis` — traces the descent into the circle of gluttony:

1. **Fate** establishes the inexorable descent.
2. **Wrath** arrives with Cerberus, the enforcer.
3. **Plague** dominates the condition: filth, stench, corruption, degradation.
4. **Desire** marks Ciacco's plea: even the damned wish to be remembered.
5. **Wrath** returns with prophecy: the coming violence of Florence.
6. **Metamorphosis** ends the canto: the landscape transforms toward the next circle.

---

## Dominant Force: Plague

The plague is not disease alone but the general condition of decay, corruption, and the loss of human measure. The music must descend, must darken, must accumulate in dissonance. Hold the beauty registers for Purgatorio. Canto VI belongs to descent.
