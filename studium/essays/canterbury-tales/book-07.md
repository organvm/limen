# Canterbury Tales — The Wife of Bath's Tale
## Desire, Sovereignty, and the Cost of a Better Question

The Wife of Bath opens the tale system of Fragment III with a prologue that is as much legal
argument as story. The arc turns on a simple reversal: desire cannot be made obedient by decree.
It must become answerable to sovereignty shared, not authority imposed. The dominant force is
therefore desire transformed by law.

## Per-Track Reasoning

**1. Offenbach — Orpheus in the Underworld: Galop infernal** *(comedy)*

The social room must reset before the Wife can begin. Offenbach handles that reset:
the room laughs to prepare for an argument that is really about authority.

**2. Handel — Water Music Suite No. 1: Overture** *(law)*

The prologue begins as self-legislation. Handel's ceremonial line supports a speaker who is
claiming the right to define the terms.

**3. Schubert — Symphony No. 8, I.** *(memory)*

Her long prelude is memory made into method. Schubert's unfinished first movement keeps
memory open rather than resolved.

**4. Brahms — Symphony No. 1: I. Allegro** *(law)*

Royal sentence turns private misconduct into public jurisdiction. Brahms holds that switch
without caricature.

**5. Beethoven — Symphony No. 7: II. Allegretto** *(fate)*

Deadline and death are administratively adjacent. The tale's pivot is the imposition of a hard
temporal law.

**6. Debussy — La mer** *(exile)*

The knight's journey is not scenic adventure but social displacement. Debussy gives exile
as open movement without center.

**7. Strauss — Also sprach Zarathustra: 'Von der großen Sehnsucht'** *(desire)*

Desire appears as method. Strauss offers an opening that is both seductive and austere, exactly
the register of learned appetite.

**8. Messiaen — Quartet for the End of Time: 'Louange'** *(divine-intervention)*

The tale's decisive turn is larger than legal technique. Messiaen gives the register of a
register changing from punishment to reinterpretation.

**9. Elgar — Cello Concerto in E minor: I. Adagio — Allegro** *(metamorphosis)*

The transformation of both husband and wife is structural, not cosmetic. The cadenza-like
motion of the cello gives that negotiated shift a focused inner logic.

**10. Ravel — Daphnis et Chloé: Suite No. 2: Lever du jour** *(desire)*

Desire finally becomes social reciprocity. Ravel's luminous lightness supports this less
violent register.

**11. Mozart — Don Giovanni: Overture** *(law)*

The community hears the tale as corrected order: desire survives, but only as chosen
sovereignty.

## The Dominant Arc of the Tale

```
comedy -> law -> memory -> law -> fate -> exile -> desire -> divine-intervention -> metamorphosis -> desire -> law
```

The dominant force is desire, but the tale's teaching is that desire must answer to shared
authority rather than private appetite.
