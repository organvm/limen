# Canterbury Tales — Tale XIX: Canon's Yeoman's Tale — Music Arc

The Canon's Yeoman's Tale is the anatomy of a system of deception that operates on hope and the desire to believe. The Canon pursues alchemical gold: each experiment fails, each failure is explained away, each explanation creates a new opportunity for investment. The yeoman is complicit until he recognizes the fraud. His tale is not moral instruction but confession: he reveals how he was trapped in the system and how he escaped. The dominant motion is the slow accumulation of lies that become indistinguishable from truth, until the point where disillusionment becomes unavoidable.

The dominant force is **fate** — understood as a system designed to perpetuate itself. The alchemy is not supernatural but material fraud, yet it works through the same principle as genuine alchemy: the transmutation of base metal (hope, gullibility, desire) into a spiritual poison (complicity, disillusionment, self-loathing).

---

## The Dominant Arc

The Canon's Yeoman's Tale moves from the description of the Canon's experiments through the attraction of clients through the multiplication of deceptions through the yeoman's recognition of the fraud through his escape to his moral on alchemy's spiritual deception. The dominant force is **fate** — the inescapability of a system that has become invisible. The yeoman's escape is not redemption but the realization that the system will continue without him, and that his only freedom is to leave. The tale offers no solution, only the acknowledgment that some systems are designed to perpetuate themselves through the desire of those trapped within them.
