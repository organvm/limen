# Canterbury Tales — Tale XVIII: Second Nun's Tale — Music Arc

The Second Nun's Tale is luminous hagiography: the life and martyrdom of Saint Cecilia, patron saint of music. Cecilia is a virtuous Christian maiden forced into pagan marriage, but protected by an angel. She converts her husband, his brother, and others through her steadfastness. She is tortured and martyred but remains unbroken. Her body incorruptible, she becomes a saint and intercessor. The tale is devotional without irony: the Second Nun believes absolutely in the truth she tells and celebrates faith, virtue, and the willingness to die for belief. The dominant motion is ascent: from earthly marriage to spiritual transformation to sainthood.

The dominant force is **salvation** — understood as the transfiguration of suffering into eternal power. The music must be genuinely beautiful and reverential, capturing the Second Nun's absolute belief and the luminosity of faith made manifest.

## The Dominant Arc

The Second Nun's Tale moves from Cecilia's protected virginity through her conversions of husband and brother through her arrest and torture to her martyrdom to her canonization as saint. The dominant force is **salvation** — understood as the path from earthly trial to eternal intercession. The tale celebrates the price paid in suffering and the reward of becoming a saint. By the end, Cecilia's death is not loss but the foundation of her eternal presence in the faithful. The music should reflect both the beauty of her faith and the reality of her suffering, understood together as the path to sainthood.
