# Sura 4: An-Nisa (Women)

An-Nisa is deeply concerned with the vulnerable—orphans, women, the weak—and establishes the detailed legal framework to protect them and structure society. The focus is on equity, inheritance, and social responsibility. The music here is architectural, measured, and precise, reflecting the divine scales of justice and the building of a fair community out of the chaos of pre-Islamic practices.

## Track Reasoning

**1. Handel — *Music for the Royal Fireworks*: La Réjouissance** — *law*

The opening immediately establishes the foundational rules of human relation, kinship, and the protection of the vulnerable. Handel's formal clarity and architecture suit the laying down of foundational societal structures.

**2. Bach — *The Well-Tempered Clavier*, Book I: Fugue in C-sharp minor** — *law*

The precise, fractional divisions of inheritance are laid out with mathematical exactitude. The intricate, interlocking voices of a complex Bach fugue reflect this perfectly balanced system of rights and shares.

**3. Wagner — *Tristan und Isolde*: Prelude** — *desire*

The sura delineates exactly who one may and may not marry, controlling and channeling human desire through divine boundaries. The chromatic longing of Wagner's prelude captures the raw force of desire that the law seeks to contain.

**4. Stravinsky — *The Rite of Spring*: Glorification of the Chosen One** — *wrath*

Strong condemnation is directed at hypocrites who sow discord and undermine the community from within. Stravinsky's rhythmic pressure and unresolved harmony mirror the dangerous instability and the harsh divine warning against them.

**5. Pärt — *Da pacem Domine*** — *supplication*

A poignant moment where the text asks why believers do not fight for the oppressed men, women, and children crying out for a savior. Pärt's exposed choral writing perfectly holds this pure, suspended plea for rescue.

**6. Beethoven — Symphony No. 7: Allegretto** — *return*

The sura closes by affirming that God does not wrong anyone by even an atom's weight, and all actions return to Him for perfect recompense. The circular form and steady tread of Beethoven's Allegretto represent this inevitable return to justice.

---

## The dominant arc of Sura 4

```
law regulating human relation → law dividing inheritance → desire channeled by limits →
wrath against internal discord → supplication of the oppressed → return to perfect justice
```

Sura 4 is the book of **law** applied to vulnerability. It constructs a societal architecture specifically designed to protect those who cannot protect themselves, turning divine will into earthly legislation.
