# Gilgamesh Tablet III: The Blessing and the Departure

Tablet III is a pause before the journey, a tablet entirely concerned with ritual, permission, and binding. The elders warn, the mother prays, the state sanctions. But the emotional fulcrum is Ninsun's adoption of Enkidu. The wild man from the steppe is formally inducted into the divine family of Uruk. This act of grace binds him inexorably to the doom of the Cedar Forest. The preparations are complete; the heroes step out of the gates.

## Per-Track Reasoning

**1. Brahms — *Symphony No. 4*, I**
The elders of Uruk try to dissuade Gilgamesh. They speak for tradition, for the safety of the city walls, for caution. Brahms's Fourth Symphony opening is grounded, complex, and deeply serious—it is the sound of institutional wisdom warning against tragic overreach.

**2. Mozart — *Don Giovanni*, Overture**
Gilgamesh laughs off their warnings. He is driven by sovereign pride and the desire for eternal fame. The Don Giovanni overture holds this exact tension: terrifying divine retribution waiting in the chords, but driven forward by an unstoppable, arrogant vitality.

**3. Gluck — *Iphigénie en Tauride***
They seek the blessing of Ninsun, the wild cow goddess. She prepares herself for ritual. Gluck provides the solemn, ceremonial weight of a priestess preparing for intercession. The ritual is formal, slow, and heavy with consequence.

**4. Bruckner — *Symphony No. 7*, I**
Ninsun goes to the roof to offer incense to Shamash, the sun god. Bruckner's long, rising tremolos and slowly building brass chorales perfectly capture the physical and spiritual ascent toward the divine realm. It is luminous orchestration rising upward.

**5. Barber — *Adagio for Strings***
Ninsun asks Shamash why he gave her son such a restless heart, and begs the sun god to protect him. Barber's Adagio is an exposed, suspended melodic line of pure petition. It is the sound of unbearable parental fear projected into the sky.

**6. Ravel — *Daphnis et Chloé***
In a profoundly moving political and spiritual act, Ninsun adopts the wild man Enkidu, elevating him to the status of her son and Gilgamesh's true brother. Ravel's daybreak music is radiant, expanding harmonic color—a life fundamentally elevated into a new dawn.

**7. Wagner — *Parsifal*, Prelude**
Ninsun places an amulet on Enkidu, marking him, binding him to her son's fate, and making him responsible for Gilgamesh's life. The slow, inexorable, holy solemnity of the Parsifal prelude seals the covenant. It is fate disguised as protection.

**8. Handel — *Royal Fireworks***
The city officials give their final blessings; the heroes depart through the gates of Uruk. The state formally sanctions their mythic endeavor. Handel provides the necessary formal clarity and architectural grandeur of a city sending its greatest weapons into the unknown.

---

## The Dominant Arc of Tablet III

```
law → kingship → sacrifice → ascent → supplication → metamorphosis → fate → law
```

The arc is a ritual ascent and descent: it begins with the laws of the elders, rises to the roof for a heartbreaking supplication to the sun god, transforms Enkidu, and ends with the formal, fatal blessing of the state. The dominant force is **supplication**—Ninsun's prayer hangs over the entirety of the expedition.
