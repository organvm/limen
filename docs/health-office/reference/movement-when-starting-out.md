# Movement when you're starting from zero

*General clinical reference. Names no patient. The office's MOVEMENT department reads this
to shape a gentle, ramped weekly plan. General guidance — **clear a new exercise program
with a clinician** first, especially with significant excess weight or any heart/joint
concern.*

---

## The principle: start low, go slow, make it automatic

The failure mode for someone starting out isn't doing too little — it's doing too much for
three days and quitting. The office plans the **smallest sustainable dose** and ramps it,
and it puts each session **on the calendar** so movement becomes a scheduled appointment,
not a daily act of will. "I'm not remembering to do yoga" is solved by the calendar, not by
motivation.

## The targets (national physical-activity guidance)

The widely-shared public-health target (US Physical Activity Guidelines / UK CMO guidance)
is **~150 minutes/week of moderate aerobic activity plus 2 days/week of muscle
strengthening** — but that's the *destination*, not week one. For someone starting out:

- **Walking is the base.** Joint-friendly, free, effective for weight and blood sugar, and
  easy to scale: start with a 10–15 minute walk most days and add a few minutes each week.
  A short walk *after meals* blunts the post-meal blood-sugar rise — a direct counter to
  the medication's metabolic pull.
- **Yoga for mobility, stress, and sleep.** Named as a preference — keep it. Gentle/beginner
  yoga 1–2×/week builds mobility and down-regulates the nervous system before bed, which
  feeds the SLEEP department.
- **Light strength, twice a week.** Bodyweight or bands — sit-to-stands, wall push-ups,
  rows. Muscle is where blood sugar gets used; it directly offsets the metabolic risk.

## The ramp

| Phase | Aerobic | Strength | Yoga |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–2 | 10–15 min walk, most days | — | 1× gentle |
| Weeks 3–4 | 15–20 min walk, most days | 1× light | 1–2× |
| Weeks 5–8 | 20–30 min walk, most days | 2× light | 1–2× |
| Building | toward 150 min/week | 2× | 1–2× |

Soreness is fine; sharp pain, chest pain, or unusual breathlessness means stop and check
with a clinician.

## What the office does with this

- Builds a **weekly movement schedule** at the person's stated level and day count, ramped
  from this table, around stated likes and limitations.
- Anchors sessions to the regimen's movement slot and **offers to put them on the
  calendar** (it proposes; the principal says go — the office doesn't fill a calendar
  uninvited).
- Keeps the dose honestly small at the start, because adherence is the whole game.

*Sources: US Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (moderate aerobic ~150 min/week +
