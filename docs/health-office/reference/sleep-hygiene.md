# Sleep hygiene — the keystone habit

*General clinical reference. Names no patient. The office's SLEEP department reads this to
build the regimen's wind-down and the nightly plan. Not a prescription — a sleep disorder
(insomnia, apnea) is evaluated and treated by a clinician.*

---

## Why this is the keystone

Sleep is upstream of almost everything else the office cares about — appetite and food
choices, blood sugar, mood, energy for movement, and the will to cook rather than order in.
Fix sleep and the other departments get easier. This is why the office treats a stable
sleep schedule as the first lever, not the last.

## The evidence-based core (AASM "Healthy Sleep Habits")

1. **Consistency beats duration.** Get up at the **same time every day** — weekends
   included. A fixed wake time anchors the body clock more powerfully than a fixed bedtime.
2. **A wind-down ramp.** Dim lights and stop stimulating activity (screens, work, hard
   conversation) in the **30–60 minutes before bed**. The brain needs a runway.
3. **Cut evening fluids.** Minimize liquids in the **hours before sleep** — the single
   most direct lever on waking up to urinate (nocturia). Pair with caffeine only before
   early afternoon and alcohol avoided near bed (both fragment sleep and worsen nocturia).
4. **Protect the environment.** Cool, dark, quiet. The bed is for sleep.
5. **Daytime sets up nighttime.** Morning light, regular daytime exercise, and no long or
   late naps all deepen night sleep.

## The nocturia tie-in

Two of these — the **fixed schedule** and the **evening fluid cutoff** — are also
first-line behavioral measures for nocturia, the same symptom in the clinical loops.
Emerging trials of CBT-I (cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia) and bedtime
adjustment show they reduce nighttime bathroom trips *and* the bother they cause. So the
office's sleep regimen does double duty: better sleep, fewer wake-ups. (If frequent
urination persists despite this, it routes back to the clinical workup — high blood sugar
and sleep apnea both cause nocturia and are checkable.)

## What the office does with this

- Sets a **wake target** and a **bed target** in the regimen and keeps them constant.
- Starts a **wind-down block** 30–60 min before the bed target.
- Sets an **evening fluid cutoff** a couple of hours before bed.
- Keeps a light **wakes-per-night tracker** so the trend is visible, not guessed.
