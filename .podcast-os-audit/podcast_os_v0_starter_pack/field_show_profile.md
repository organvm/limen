# Field Show Profile v0

## Distinction from the flagship

The flagship is a controlled salon. The field show is an **encounter inside the guest's world**.

Do not carry a fake studio into someone else's space. The suitcase carries capture, audio, metadata, and a small identity object—not décor.

## Format engine

**PLACE → OBJECT → INTERVENTION → ARTIFACT**

1. **Place:** establish why this location matters.
2. **Object:** the guest shows something that carries history, method, or conflict.
3. **Intervention:** the host asks them to demonstrate, change, test, rank, build, destroy, or reinterpret something.
4. **Artifact:** leave with a new object, text, prediction, route, sound, image, or question.

## Portable camera grammar

- environmental master placed to reveal the room;
- guest angle;
- host angle;
- optional roaming/object camera;
- independent local audio on every participant;
- synchronization slate at the start and after any interruption.

The wide-angle character of small action cameras should be treated as an intentional observational language, not disguised as conventional studio cinema.

## Shared backend

The field show uses the same:
- guest graph;
- relationship history;
- booking workflow;
- research system;
- releases and permissions;
- segment library;
- commitment ledger;
- post-production metadata;
- clip and publication pipeline.

It receives a separate `show_id`, outreach voice, visual profile, segment weights, and publishing calendar.

## Release doctrine

Design both shows now, but do not begin two rigid weekly schedules simultaneously. The field show can begin as irregular, high-value films while the flagship proves the operating cadence.
