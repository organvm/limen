# Visual Language v0 — Portable Salon Kernel

## Central doctrine

**The room, not the rig.**

The audience should perceive an intimate, intentional encounter—not a podcast set. Take the principle of a private lounge conversation without copying another show's décor.

## Non-negotiables

- No visible microphones, boom arms, shock mounts, or headphone clutter.
- No desk separating hosts and guest.
- No large neon show logo.
- No generic RGB wash.
- No symmetrical “creator studio” composition by default.
- No rebuilding the three studios before the format proves itself.

## Low-impact studio skin

The show should travel between existing rooms using a repeatable visual kernel:

- two or three chairs angled toward one another rather than directly toward camera;
- one low table with only objects that have narrative function;
- one warm practical light visible in frame;
- one restrained programmable accent or projected texture;
- one physical segment deck or artifact box;
- one episode-specific object, image, or generated symbol;
- controlled negative space and a deep background.

Minimalism means **few objects with purpose**, not an empty room.

## Camera grammar

1. **Environmental master** — establishes bodies, room, and spatial relation.
2. **Ari single** — medium or medium-close, preserving enough environment to avoid a broadcast look.
3. **Guest single** — matched in visual weight, not necessarily perfect symmetry.
4. **Artifact/object angle** — optional overhead or detail camera used only when the episode produces or examines something.

Cuts should follow changes in attention, tension, or segment—not every sentence. The edit may use restrained digital push-ins, but should not simulate frantic short-form pacing.

## Audio grammar

- concealed lavalier on every participant;
- overhead or out-of-frame directional microphone as safety;
- room tone recorded for every setup;
- wardrobe and rustle check before the guest enters;
- isolated tracks for each speaker.

Hidden audio must be more redundant, not less rigorous.

## Light and palette

- soft, dimensional face light;
- warm practical source in the room;
- darker background with selective depth;
- neutral physical palette: charcoal, bone, wood, aged metal, restrained textile;
- one changing signal color or graphic texture per episode.

**Rule:** the physical room is calm; the graphic layer may become strange.

## Graphic language

- kinetic type rather than persistent lower thirds;
- segment titles appear briefly and then disappear;
- episode-specific glyph, diagram, or artifact derived from the conversation;
- thumbnails describe the episode tension, not merely list the guest's name;
- all three studio cities share typography, pacing, title behavior, and artifact treatment.

## Evolution path

### v0 — Proof
Furniture repositioning, hidden audio, controlled lighting, segment deck, and unified graphics.

### v1 — Recognition
Custom artifact table, repeated sculptural object, replicated lighting package in all three cities.

### v2 — Dedicated environment
Transformable room whose light, projection, and objects change by episode while preserving the same visual grammar.
