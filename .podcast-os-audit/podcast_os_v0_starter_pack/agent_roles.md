# Agent and Service Roles v0

These are internal capabilities, not fake public employees.

## 1. Network Cartographer
Builds a permissioned graph from previous guests, public professional history, representatives, weak ties, studio activity, and approved contact data.

## 2. Relationship Capital Governor
Scores social cost, identifies protected relationships, recommends the least socially expensive credible route, and blocks automatic outreach from personal accounts.

## 3. Guest Thesis Architect
Produces: why this guest, why now, why this show, the central tension, the likely artifact, and the segment plan.

## 4. Producer Outreach Desk
Drafts correspondence, verifies personalized claims, manages the shared inbox, classifies replies, and escalates ambiguity.

## 5. Studio Router
Matches accepted guests to LA, NYC, or Austin using guest travel, host availability, room capacity, crew, and production requirements.

## 6. Episode Designer
Generates the research brief, selects segments, identifies receipts, writes the rundown, and marks potential clip boundaries.

## 7. Commercial Break Lab
Converts sponsor requirements into truthful, disclosed, entertaining ad concepts and production plans.

## 8. Asset Producer
Creates edit markers, chapters, title options, thumbnail concepts, clips, stills, transcripts, descriptions, and guest delivery packages.

## 9. Relationship Steward
Tracks promises, thank-yous, gifts, publication notices, referrals, future episode ideas, and do-not-contact status.

## 10. Operations Analyst
Measures booking conversion, time-to-book, segment retention, clip conversion, returning audience, guest satisfaction, sponsor renewal, and human correction rates.

## Ari interface

Ari receives one digest rather than an inbox. Each card must support:

- APPROVE
- REJECT
- PROTECT RELATIONSHIP
- ADD PERSONAL NOTE

Default target: fewer than ten minutes of Ari operational work per week.
