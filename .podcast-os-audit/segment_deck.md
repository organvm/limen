# Segment Deck v0

Use **three fixed segments** in every episode and select **one or two rotating segments** based on the guest. The format should be recognizable without becoming a game show.

## Fixed segments

### 1. The Claim
The guest states one consequential proposition: something they believe, something their field gets wrong, or a decision they would defend.

**Output:** the episode thesis, title language, and first major clip.

### 2. The Stress Test
The hosts test the claim through contradiction, counterexample, comedy, research, or a designed scenario.

**Host split:** Ari applies instinct and friction; the co-host applies structure, evidence, and synthesis.

**Output:** conflict, surprise, and the strongest conversational clips.

### 3. The Artifact
The guest and hosts produce something that did not exist before the recording: a rule, prediction, ranking, plan, diagram, short text, question, design, or commitment.

**Output:** a visual object for the episode, a social asset, and a reason the conversation mattered.

## Rotating segments

### Receipts
Present a verified quotation, old clip, abandoned project, contradictory statement, or public prediction. Ask whether it still holds.

### Object Lesson
The guest brings or selects an object that reveals how they work, think, remember, or fail.

### Explain It to Ari
A complex idea must survive plain language and skepticism. Ari is not framed as stupid; he is the compression test.

### Build the Worst Version
Design the most destructive, absurd, or corrupted version of the guest's industry, idea, or product. Then identify which elements already exist.

### Future Headline
The guest writes a plausible headline from five years in the future and explains the causal chain.

### Opposite Chair
The guest must defend the strongest version of the position opposite their own.

### The Unfinished Thing
Revisit a project, belief, relationship, or ambition the guest has not resolved.

### The Question for the Next Guest
The guest leaves one question for a future participant and may name the kind of person—not necessarily a friend—who should answer it.

## Segment selection rubric

Choose a segment only when it:
- exposes a real tension;
- changes the emotional or intellectual mode;
- can produce a discrete clip;
- gives the guest something interesting to do;
- does not humiliate or ambush them;
- remains understandable in audio-only form.
